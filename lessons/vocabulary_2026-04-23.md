# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-23

Hi there! Let's learn some new words.

## 1. **Deploy** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /dɪˈplɔɪ/
**Definition:** To move troops or equipment into position for military action; to use something effectively.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 部署; 有效運用

**Example 1:**
The police decided to deploy more officers to the protest site.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
警方決定部署更多警員到示威現場。

**Example 2:**
The company plans to deploy new software across all departments.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
公司計劃喺所有部門部署新嘅軟件。

## 2. **Tackle** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtækəl/
**Definition:** To make determined efforts to deal with a problem or difficult task.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 處理; 應付

**Example 1:**
The government is trying to tackle the issue of homelessness.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府正嘗試處理露宿者嘅問題。

**Example 2:**
He needs to tackle his fear of public speaking.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢需要應付佢對公開演講嘅恐懼。

## 3. **Urge** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ɜːdʒ/
**Definition:** To try earnestly or persistently to persuade (someone) to do something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 敦促; 催促

**Example 1:**
Environmental groups are urging the government to take action on climate change.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
環保團體正敦促政府採取行動應對氣候變化。

**Example 2:**
I urge you to reconsider your decision.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我敦促你重新考慮你嘅決定。

## 4. **Crosshairs** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈkrɒs.heəz/
**Definition:** The intersection of the lines in a telescopic sight or other sighting device, used for aiming. Also, a situation where someone is under attack or facing scrutiny.
**Cantonese Meaning:** (瞄準鏡嘅)十字線; 成為攻擊或審查嘅目標

**Example 1:**
The company found itself in the crosshairs of consumer rights groups.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司發現自己成為消費者權益團體嘅攻擊目標。

**Example 2:**
The politician is in the crosshairs after his controversial remarks.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰位政治人物喺佢嘅爭議性言論之後成為眾矢之的。

## 5. **Standoff** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈstænd.ɒf/
**Definition:** A deadlock or confrontation between opposing parties.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 對峙; 僵局

**Example 1:**
The hostage situation led to a tense standoff between the police and the criminals.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
人質事件導致警察同罪犯之間嘅緊張對峙。

**Example 2:**
Negotiations ended in a standoff, with neither side willing to compromise.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
談判以僵局告終，雙方都唔願意妥協。

## 6. **Extortion** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪkˈstɔːʃən/
**Definition:** The practice of obtaining something, especially money, through force or threats.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 勒索; 敲詐

**Example 1:**
He was arrested and charged with extortion.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢因為勒索而被捕同埋控告。

**Example 2:**
The businessman was a victim of an extortion attempt.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰位商人係勒索未遂嘅受害者。

## 7. **Deadlock** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdedlɒk/
**Definition:** A situation, typically involving opposing parties, in which no progress can be made.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 僵局; 膠著狀態

**Example 1:**
The negotiations reached a deadlock after several hours of discussion.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
經過幾個鐘頭嘅討論，談判陷入僵局。

**Example 2:**
The political situation is in a deadlock, with no sign of a resolution.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
政治局勢陷入僵局，冇任何解決嘅跡象。
