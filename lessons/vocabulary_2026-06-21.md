# Daily English Lesson - 2026-06-21

## 1. **Evaporating** (Adjective/Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪˈvæp.ə.reɪ.tɪŋ/
**Definition:** Disappearing or fading away quickly, often used to describe support or hope.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 蒸發緊，消散緊（形容支持度或信心消失）

**Example 1:**
Public support for the minister is evaporating as the economic situation worsens.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
隨住經濟狀況惡化，公眾對部長嘅支持正不斷消散。

**Example 2:**
Any hope of a peaceful resolution seemed to be evaporating by the hour.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
睇嚟和平解決嘅希望正逐個鐘咁消失。

## 2. **Monarch** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈmɒn.ək/
**Definition:** A king, queen, or emperor who is the ruler of a state.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 君主（國王或女王）

**Example 1:**
The monarch is expected to attend the opening of the new hospital later this month.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
預計君主會喺今個月尾出席新醫院嘅開幕儀式。

**Example 2:**
Historically, the monarch had absolute power over the kingdom.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺歷史上，君主對王國擁有絕對嘅權力。

## 3. **Force** (Noun - in context of 'come into force')
**Pronunciation:** /fɔːs/
**Definition:** To become valid or effective, usually referring to a law, rule, or warning.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 生效（用於法例或警告）

**Example 1:**
The new extreme heat warning will come into force at midnight tonight.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
最新嘅極端高溫警報將會喺今晚午夜生效。

**Example 2:**
These regulations were brought into force to ensure worker safety.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢啲規例係為了確保員工安全而開始生效。

## 4. **Humanitarian** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /hjuːˌmæn.ɪˈteə.ri.ən/
**Definition:** Involved in or connected with improving people's lives and reducing suffering.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 人道（主義）嘅

**Example 1:**
The UN has requested urgent access to deliver humanitarian aid to the region.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
聯合國已要求緊急進入該地區，以提供人道救援。

**Example 2:**
He dedicated his entire career to humanitarian work across the globe.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢將整個職業生涯都貢獻畀全球嘅人道工作。

## 5. **Convoy** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈkɒn.vɔɪ/
**Definition:** A group of vehicles traveling together, often for safety or protection.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 車隊（通常指一組車一齊行）

**Example 1:**
A humanitarian convoy was allowed to pass through the border yesterday.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
一支人道救援車隊尋日獲准通過邊境。

**Example 2:**
The military convoy moved slowly through the narrow village streets.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
軍事車隊慢慢穿過狹窄嘅村莊街道。

## 6. **Brutal** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbruː.təl/
**Definition:** Cruel, violent, and completely without feelings.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 殘酷嘅，無情嘅

**Example 1:**
The country is currently suffering from a brutal and long-lasting internal conflict.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰個國家目前正飽受一場殘酷且持久嘅內部衝突。

**Example 2:**
It was a brutal winter, with temperatures staying well below freezing for months.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個冬天非常殘酷，氣溫連續幾個月都遠低於冰點。

## 7. **Profound** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /prəˈfaʊnd/
**Definition:** Felt or experienced very strongly or in an extreme way; showing great knowledge.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 深遠嘅，深刻嘅

**Example 1:**
The rise of artificial intelligence will have a profound impact on our daily lives.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
人工智能嘅興起將會對我哋嘅日常生活產生深遠影響。

**Example 2:**
She made a profound observation about the nature of modern society.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢對現代社會嘅本質提出咗一個深刻嘅見解。
