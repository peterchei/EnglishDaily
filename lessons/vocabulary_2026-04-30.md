# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-30

## 1. **Mojo** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈməʊ.dʒəʊ/
**Definition:** A quality that makes you very successful and full of energy.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 魅力, 幹勁, 精神

**Example 1:**
The team seemed to have lost their mojo after their star player got injured.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
自從佢哋嘅明星球員受傷之後，球隊似乎失去咗佢哋嘅幹勁。

**Example 2:**
That holiday in the sun really helped me get my mojo back.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個陽光假期真係幫我恢復咗我嘅精神。

## 2. **Briefed** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /briːft/
**Definition:** To give someone information about something, especially before they do something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 簡報, 匯報, 告知

**Example 1:**
The president was briefed on the latest developments in the Iran war.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
總統聽取咗關於伊朗戰爭嘅最新發展嘅簡報。

**Example 2:**
The lawyers briefed their client before the court hearing.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
律師喺法庭聆訊之前向佢哋嘅客戶匯報咗情況。

## 3. **Sparred** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /spɑːrd/
**Definition:** To argue with someone, especially in a friendly or playful way.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 爭論, 辯論, 鬥嘴

**Example 1:**
The politicians sparred over the proposed budget cuts.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政治家們就提議嘅預算削減問題進行咗辯論。

**Example 2:**
My brother and I often spar about which football team is better.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我同我阿哥經常爭論邊隊足球隊更好。

## 4. **Reform** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /rɪˈfɔːm/
**Definition:** The improvement or amendment of what is wrong, corrupt, or unsatisfactory.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 改革, 改良, 改善

**Example 1:**
The government is planning major tax reforms.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府正計劃重大嘅稅務改革。

**Example 2:**
Prison reform is desperately needed in this country.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢個國家急需監獄改革。

## 5. **Overturn** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌəʊ.vəˈtɜːn/
**Definition:** To officially decide that a previous legal decision was incorrect.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 推翻 (裁決), 撤銷

**Example 1:**
The appeal court overturned the original verdict.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
上訴法院推翻咗原判。

**Example 2:**
He is trying to overturn his dismissal from the company.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢試圖推翻佢被公司解僱嘅決定。

## 6. **Combative** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈkɒm.bə.tɪv/
**Definition:** Ready and eager to fight or argue.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好鬥嘅, 爭強好勝嘅

**Example 1:**
He was in a combative mood and started arguing with everyone.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢當時情緒好鬥，開始同所有人爭論。

**Example 2:**
The interview became combative as the politician defended his policies.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
隨着政治家捍衛佢嘅政策，採訪變得充滿對抗性。
