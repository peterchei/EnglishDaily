1. **Brushing** (Noun/Gerund)
**Pronunciation**: /ˈbrʌʃɪŋ/
**Definition**: A deceitful technique used in e-commerce to boost a seller's ratings by sending unsolicited packages to people and then writing fake positive reviews in their name.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 「刷單」騙局。電商賣家為咗推高評價，將啲唔係你買嘅包裹寄畀你，然後用你個名寫假嘅好評。
**Example 1**: I was confused when a mystery parcel arrived, but then I realized it was a case of brushing.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 我收到個神秘包裹嗰陣好摸不着頭腦，之後先發現原來係刷單騙局。
**Example 2**: Consumer groups have warned the public to be cautious of brushing scams during the holiday season.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 消費者權益組織提醒市民，喺節日期間要提防刷單騙局。

2. **Chutzpah** (Noun)
**Pronunciation**: /ˈhʊtspə/
**Definition**: Extreme self-confidence or audacity (often used in a way that is both impressive and shocking).
**Cantonese Explanation**: 夠膽死、厚顏無恥、驚人嘅膽量。通常形容一啲令人佩服但又覺得好離譜嘅自信。
**Example 1**: It took a lot of chutzpah for him to ask for a promotion after only two weeks on the job.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 佢先返咗兩星期工就要求升職，真係幾夠膽死。
**Example 2**: I admire the chutzpah of that restaurant owner who opened right next to a famous Michelin-starred chef.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 我好佩服嗰間餐廳老闆嘅膽量，竟然敢喺米芝蓮名廚隔離度開鋪。

3. **Misfire** (Noun/Verb)
**Pronunciation**: /ˌmɪsˈfaɪər/
**Definition**: A failure to produce the intended result; or for a gun or engine to fail to fire or start correctly.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 失靈、失手、事與願違、哑火。指一件嘢無達到預期嘅效果，或者機器發動唔到。
**Example 1**: The marketing campaign was a bit of a misfire and didn't attract many new customers.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 嗰個市場推廣活動有啲失手，吸納唔到好多新客。
**Example 2**: The new film was expected to be a hit, but it proved to be a critical misfire.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 嗰套新戲本來預計會大受歡迎，點知影評反應好一般，算係失咗手。

4. **Hedonistic** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation**: /ˌhiːdəˈnɪstɪk/
**Definition**: Engaged in the pursuit of pleasure; sensually self-indulgent.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 享樂主義嘅、追求快感嘅。形容一個人淨係顧住食飲玩樂。
**Example 1**: In his youth, he led a very hedonistic lifestyle, traveling the world and attending parties.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 佢後生嗰陣過住非常享樂主義嘅生活，環遊世界同埋去唔同嘅派對。
**Example 2**: The island is known for its hedonistic atmosphere and vibrant nightlife.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 呢個島以享樂嘅氛圍同埋精彩嘅夜生活聞名。

5. **Atonal** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation**: /eɪˈtoʊnl/
**Definition**: Music that is not written in any particular key or scale (often sounding "clashing" or experimental).
**Cantonese Explanation**: 無調性嘅（音樂）。即係唔跟傳統音階寫嘅音樂，聽落可能有啲唔協調或者實驗性。
**Example 1**: Some people find atonal music difficult to listen to because it lacks a clear melody.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 有啲人覺得無調性音樂好難聽，因為佢無一段清晰嘅旋律。
**Example 2**: The composer is famous for his complex and atonal orchestral works.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 呢位作曲家以佢複雜同埋無調性嘅管弦樂作品而聞名。

6. **Road test** (Noun/Verb)
**Pronunciation**: /ˈroʊd test/
**Definition**: A test of a vehicle's performance on the road; or more generally, to test a new product or idea in real-life conditions.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 路試、實測、實際測試。本來指試車，而家廣泛指喺實際環境下測試新產品或者諗法。
**Example 1**: We decided to road test the new camping gear in the back garden before our trip.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 我哋決定喺去旅行之前，先喺後花園實測一下啲新露營裝備。
**Example 2**: The journalist is going to road test the latest smartphone to see if the battery really lasts all day.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 嗰個記者準備實測最新款嘅手機，睇下個電池係咪真係可以用到全日。

7. **Creaky** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation**: /ˈkriːki/
**Definition**: Making a harsh, high-pitched sound (like an old door or floorboard); or old-fashioned and no longer working well.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 吱吱嘎嘎響嘅、老舊嘅、過時嘅。可以形容啲物件好舊有聲，或者形容啲制度、諗法好老土。
**Example 1**: I tried to creep out of the room, but the creaky floorboards gave me away.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 我想靜靜雞行出房，但係吱吱嘎嘎響嘅地板出賣咗我。
**Example 2**: The company’s creaky IT system needs an urgent upgrade to keep up with modern demands.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 呢間公司個老舊 IT 系統需要盡快升級，先至可以應付到現代嘅需求。
