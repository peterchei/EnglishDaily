# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-05

Hi there! Today we'll learn some useful English words.

## 1. **Cheeky** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtʃiːki/
**Definition:** Impudent or disrespectful in a playful or amusing way.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 厚面皮 (hau5 min6 pei4), 曳 (jai6)

**Example 1:**
That's a rather cheeky remark!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰句嘢幾厚面皮喎！ (go2 geoi3 je5 gei2 hau5 min6 pei4 wo3!)

**Example 2:**
He gave me a cheeky grin.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢對住我曳曳咁笑。 (keoi5 deoi3 zyu6 ngo5 jai6 jai6 gam2 siu3.)

## 2. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed or upset.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望 (hou2 sat1 mong6), 心痛 (sam1 tung3)

**Example 1:**
I was gutted when I didn't get the job.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我攞唔到份工，真係好失望。(ngo5 lo2 m4 dou2 fan6 gung1, zan1 hai6 hou2 sat1 mong6.)

**Example 2:**
She was absolutely gutted to hear the news.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢聽到嗰個消息，真係心痛死。 (keoi5 ting1 dou2 go2 go3 siu1 sik1, zan1 hai6 sam1 tung3 sei2.)

## 3. **Knackered** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnækərd/
**Definition:** Exhausted; very tired.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好攰 (hou2 gui6), 殘 (caan4)

**Example 1:**
I'm absolutely knackered after that workout.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
做完嗰個運動之後，我真係好攰。(zou6 jyun4 go2 go3 wan6 dung6 zi1 hau6, ngo5 zan1 hai6 hou2 gui6.)

**Example 2:**
She looks completely knackered.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢睇落去殘到死。(keoi5 tai2 lok6 heoi3 caan4 dou3 sei2.)

## 4. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒi/
**Definition:** Suspicious, unreliable, or potentially dangerous.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑嘅 (ho2 ji4 ge3), 唔可靠嘅 (m4 ho2 kao3 ge3), 危險嘅 (ngai4 him2 ge3)

**Example 1:**
That restaurant looks a bit dodgy.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰間餐廳睇落去有啲可疑。(go2 gaan1 caan1 ting1 tai2 lok6 heoi3 jau5 di1 ho2 ji4.)

**Example 2:**
I wouldn't trust him; he seems a bit dodgy.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我唔會信佢，佢好似有啲唔可靠。(ngo5 m4 wui5 seon3 keoi5, keoi5 hou2 ci5 jau5 di1 m4 ho2 kao3.)

## 5. **Sorted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsɔːrtɪd/
**Definition:** Organized, arranged, or dealt with successfully.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 搞掂 (gaau2 dim6), 安排好咗 (aan1 paai4 hou2 zo2)

**Example 1:**
Everything is sorted for the trip.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
所有嘢都安排好曬喇，準備去旅行。(so2 jau5 je5 dou1 aan1 paai4 hou2 saai3 laa3, zeon2 bei6 heoi3 leoi5 haang4.)

**Example 2:**
"Have you booked the tickets?" "Yes, it's all sorted."
**Cantonese Example 2:**
「你book咗啲飛未呀？」「係呀，搞掂曬喇。」("nei5 book1 zo2 di1 fei1 mei6 aa3?" "hai6 aa3, gaau2 dim6 saai3 laa3.")

## 6. **Taking the piss** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkɪŋ ðə pɪs/
**Definition:** To tease or make fun of someone in a playful or disrespectful way.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 玩嘢 (waan2 je5), 攞嚟搞 (lo2 lai4 gaau2), 寸 (cyun3)

**Example 1:**
Are you taking the piss out of my new haircut?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你係咪玩我個新髮型呀？(nei5 hai6 mai5 waan2 ngo5 go3 san1 faat3 jing4 aa3?)

**Example 2:**
He's always taking the piss out of his friends.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢成日攞啲朋友嚟搞。(keoi5 seng4 jat6 lo2 di1 pang4 jau5 lai4 gaau2.)
