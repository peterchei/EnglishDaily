# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-08

Hi there! Today we'll learn some useful British English words and phrases.

## 1. **Cheers** (Exclamation)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** A friendly expression used to mean "thank you," "goodbye," or "good luck".
**Cantonese Meaning:** 多謝 (do1 ze6), 再見 (zoi3 gin3), 祝你好運 (zuk1 nei5 hou2 wan6)

**Example 1:**
Cheers for helping me with my bags.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
唔該你幫我拎行李。
(m4 goi1 nei5 bong1 ngo5 ling1 hang4 lei5.)

**Example 2:**
Cheers, see you next week!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
拜拜，下個星期見！
(baai1 baai3, haa6 go3 sing1 kei4 gin3!)

## 2. **Queue** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /kjuː/
**Definition:** A line of people waiting for something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 隊 (deoi2), 排隊 (paai4 deoi2)

**Example 1:**
There's a long queue outside the bakery this morning.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今朝麵包舖出面有條長龍。
(gam1 ziu1 min6 baau1 pou3 chut1 min6 jau5 tiu4 coeng4 lung4.)

**Example 2:**
We had to join the queue for the bus.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我哋要排隊等巴士。
(ngo5 dei6 jiu3 paai4 deoi2 dang2 baa1 si2.)

## 3. **Rubbish** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈrʌbɪʃ/
**Definition:** Waste material; trash. Also, something that is considered to be of very poor quality.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 垃圾 (laap6 saap3), 廢話 (fai3 waa2)

**Example 1:**
Please take the rubbish out.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
麻煩你攞啲垃圾出去。
(maa4 faan4 nei5 lo2 di1 laap6 saap3 chut1 heoi3.)

**Example 2:**
That movie was complete rubbish!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰套戲真係廢到爆！
(go2 tou3 hei3 zan1 hai6 fai3 dou3 baau3!)

## 4. **Brilliant** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbrɪlɪənt/
**Definition:** Extremely intelligent or talented; also, very good or impressive.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 叻 (lek1), 好棒 (hou2 paang5)

**Example 1:**
She's a brilliant student.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢係一個好叻嘅學生。
(keoi5 hai6 jat1 go3 hou2 lek1 ge3 hok6 sang1.)

**Example 2:**
That's a brilliant idea!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢個諗法真係好棒！
(ni1 go3 nam2 faat3 zan1 hai6 hou2 paang5!)

## 5. **Knackered** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnækəd/
**Definition:** Very tired; exhausted. (Informal, British English)
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好攰 (hou2 gui6), 累到死 (leoi6 dou3 sei2)

**Example 1:**
I'm absolutely knackered after that long walk.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
行咗咁耐，我真係好攰。
(haang4 zo2 gam3 noi6, ngo5 zan1 hai6 hou2 gui6.)

**Example 2:**
He looks completely knackered.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢睇落去累到死咁。
(keoi5 tai2 lok6 heoi3 leoi6 dou3 sei2 gam2.)

## 6. **To fancy** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfænsi/
**Definition:** To want something or want to do something; or to be attracted to someone.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 想要 (seung2 jiu3), 鍾意 (zung1 ji3)

**Example 1:**
Do you fancy going to the cinema tonight?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你今晚想唔想去睇戲？
(nei5 gam1 maan1 seung2 m4 seung2 heoi3 tai2 hei3?)

**Example 2:**
I think he fancies her.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我覺得佢鍾意佢。
(ngo5 gok3 dak1 keoi5 zung1 ji3 keoi5.)
