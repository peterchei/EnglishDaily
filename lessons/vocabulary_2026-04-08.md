# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-08

## N. **Ceasefire** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsiːsfaɪər/
**Definition:** A temporary suspension of fighting, typically one during which peace talks take place; a truce.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 停火

**Example 1:**
The fragile ceasefire is holding despite some minor violations.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
即使有一些輕微嘅違規行為，脆弱嘅停火協議仍然維持緊。

**Example 2:**
Negotiators are working to turn the ceasefire into a permanent peace agreement.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
談判代表正努力將停火協議變成永久和平協議。

## N. **Truce** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /truːs/
**Definition:** An agreement between enemies or opponents to stop fighting for a certain time.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 休戰

**Example 1:**
The two armies declared a truce to allow for the evacuation of civilians.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
兩軍宣布休戰，以便疏散平民。

**Example 2:**
They called a temporary truce to discuss the terms of surrender.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢哋宣布暫時休戰，嚟討論投降嘅條款。

## N. **Halted** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈhɔːltɪd/
**Definition:** To bring or come to an abrupt stop.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 停止咗

**Example 1:**
Production at the factory was halted due to the power outage.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
由於停電，工廠嘅生產停止咗。

**Example 2:**
The train was halted by a signal failure.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
火車因為信號故障而停止咗。

## N. **Plunges** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /plʌndʒɪz/
**Definition:** To decrease suddenly and greatly; plummet.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 暴跌

**Example 1:**
The stock market plunges after the unexpected news.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
喺意料之外嘅消息之後，股市暴跌。

**Example 2:**
Oil prices plunges after the ceasefire agreement was announced.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
停火協議宣布後，油價暴跌。

## N. **Headline** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈhedlaɪn/
**Definition:** A heading at the top of an article or page in a newspaper or magazine.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 標題

**Example 1:**
The headline read "Peace Talks Break Down".
**Cantonese Example 1:**
標題寫著“和平談判破裂”。

**Example 2:**
Did you see the headline about the new tax laws?
**Cantonese Example 2:**
你有冇睇到關於新稅法嘅頭條新聞？

## N. **Criticism** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/
**Definition:** The expression of disapproval of someone or something based on perceived faults or mistakes.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 批評

**Example 1:**
The government faced strong criticism for its handling of the crisis.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府因為處理危機嘅方式受到強烈批評。

**Example 2:**
She received constructive criticism from her manager, which helped her improve.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢收到經理嘅建設性批評，幫助佢改進。

## N. **Fragile** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfrædʒaɪl/
**Definition:** Easily broken or damaged; delicate.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 脆弱嘅

**Example 1:**
The antique vase was very fragile.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
呢個古董花瓶非常脆弱。

**Example 2:**
The ceasefire agreement is fragile and could collapse at any moment.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
停火協議好脆弱，隨時都有可能崩潰。
