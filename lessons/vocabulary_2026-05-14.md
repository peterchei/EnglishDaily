## 1. **Plotting** (Verb - gerund or present participle)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈplɒtɪŋ/
**Definition:** Secretly planning something, usually something dishonest or harmful.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 密謀，策劃 (maat6 mau4, caak3 waak6)

**Example 1:**
He was accused of plotting against the company's CEO.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢被指控密謀對付公司嘅行政總裁。 (keoi5 bei6 zi2 hung3 mat6 mau4 deoi3 fu6 gung1 si1 ge3 hang4 zing3 zung2 coi3.)

**Example 2:**
The rebels are plotting to overthrow the government.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
叛軍密謀推翻政府。 (bun6 gwan1 mat6 mau4 teoi1 faan1 zing3 fu2.)

## 2. **Would-be** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈwʊd biː/
**Definition:** Desiring or attempting to be.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 想要成為嘅，有志成為嘅 (soeng2 jiu3 sing4 wai4 ge3, jau5 zi3 sing4 wai4 ge3)

**Example 1:**
The would-be prime minister is giving a speech later today.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個想做首相嘅人今日晏啲會發表演講。 (go3 soeng2 zou6 sau2 soeng3 ge3 jan4 gam1 jat6 aan3 di1 wui faat3 biu2 jin2 gong2.)

**Example 2:**
Many would-be entrepreneurs find it difficult to secure funding.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
好多有志成為企業家嘅人都覺得攞資金好難。 (hou2 do1 jau5 zi3 sing4 wai4 kei5 jip6 gaa1 ge3 jan4 dou1 gok3 dak1 lo2 zi1 gam1 hou2 naan4.)

## 3. **High-stakes** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌhaɪ ˈsteɪks/
**Definition:** Involving a great risk or reward.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 高風險嘅，高賭注嘅 (gou1 fung1 him2 ge3, gou1 dou2 zyu3 ge3)

**Example 1:**
The negotiations between the two countries are high-stakes.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
兩國之間嘅談判係高風險嘅。 (loeng5 gwok3 zi1 gaan1 ge3 taam4 pun3 hai6 gou1 fung1 him2 ge3.)

**Example 2:**
This is a high-stakes game, and we can't afford to lose.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢個係一個高賭注嘅遊戲，我哋輸唔起。 (ni1 go3 hai6 jat1 go3 gou1 dou2 zyu3 ge3 jau4 hei3, ngo5 dei6 syu1 m4 hei2.)

## 4. **Inevitable** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪˈnevɪtəbl/
**Definition:** Certain to happen; unavoidable.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 必然嘅，無可避免嘅 (bit1 jin4 ge3, mou4 ho2 bei6 min5 ge3)

**Example 1:**
It's inevitable that prices will rise after the new taxes are introduced.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
推出新稅後，價格上漲係必然嘅。 (teoi1 ceot1 san1 seoi3 hau6, gaa3 gaak3 soeng5 zoeng2 hai6 bit1 jin4 ge3.)

**Example 2:**
Change is inevitable in a fast-paced industry like technology.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺科技咁快嘅行業入面，改變係無可避免嘅。 (hai2 fo1 gei6 gam3 faai3 ge3 hang4 jip6 jap6 min6, goi2 bin3 hai6 mou4 ho2 bei6 min5 ge3.)

## 5. **Endures** (Verb - third person singular present)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪnˈdjʊəz/
**Definition:** Remains in existence; lasts.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 持續，存留 (ci4 zuk6, cyun4 lau4)

**Example 1:**
The artist's legacy endures long after his death.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
呢位藝術家嘅遺產喺佢死後仍然持續存在。 (ni1 wai2 ngai6 seot1 gaa1 ge3 wai4 caan2 hai2 keoi5 sei2 hau6 jing4 jin4 ci4 zuk6 cyun4 zoi6.)

**Example 2:**
Despite the hardships, their friendship endures.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
儘管有困難，佢哋嘅友誼仍然持續。 (zeon2 gun2 jau5 kwan3 naan4, keoi5 dei6 ge3 jau5 ji5 jing4 jin4 ci4 zuk6.)

## 6. **Dupe** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /djuːp/
**Definition:** Deceive; trick.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 欺騙 (hei1 pin3)

**Example 1:**
They duped her into giving them her credit card details.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢哋欺騙佢，令佢俾咗信用卡資料佢哋。 (keoi5 dei6 hei1 pin3 keoi5, ling6 keoi5 bei2 zo2 seon3 jung6 kaat1 zi1 liu2 keoi5 dei6.)

**Example 2:**
The scammer duped thousands of people out of their savings.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
個騙徒欺騙咗成千上萬嘅人，攞走咗佢哋嘅儲蓄。 (go3 pin3 tou4 hei1 pin3 zo2 sing4 cin1 soeng6 maan6 ge3 jan4, lo2 zau2 zo2 keoi5 dei6 ge3 cyu5 huk1.)

## 7. **Recall** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /rɪˈkɔːl/
**Definition:** Officially order the return of a faulty product.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 召回 (siu6 wui4)

**Example 1:**
The company had to recall thousands of cars due to a safety issue.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司因為安全問題要召回幾千架車。 (gaan1 gung1 si1 jan1 wai6 on1 cyun4 man6 tai4 jiu3 siu6 wui4 gei2 cin1 gaa3 ce1.)

**Example 2:**
They issued a product recall after reports of defects.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺收到缺陷報告後，佢哋發布咗產品召回通知。 (hai2 sau1 dou2 kyut3 haam6 bou3 gou3 hau6, keoi5 dei6 faat3 bou3 zo2 caan2 ban2 siu6 wui4 tung1 zi1.)
