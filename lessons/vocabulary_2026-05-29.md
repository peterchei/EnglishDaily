## 1. **Framework** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfreɪmwɜːk/
**Definition:** A basic structure underlying a system, concept, or text.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 框架 (kwaang1 gaa2)

**Example 1:**
The framework of the peace agreement was agreed upon after months of negotiations.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
經過幾個月嘅談判，和平協議嘅框架終於達成咗。
(ging1 gwo3 gei2 go3 jyut6 ge3 taam4 pun3, wo4 ping4 hip3 ji5 ge3 kwaang1 gaa2 zung1 jyu1 daat6 sing4 zo2.)

**Example 2:**
We need to establish a solid framework for our new project.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我哋需要為我哋嘅新項目建立一個穩固嘅框架。
(ngo5 dei6 seoi1 jiu3 wai4 ngo5 dei6 ge3 san1 hong6 muk6 gin3 lap6 jat1 go3 wan2 gu3 ge3 kwaang1 gaa2.)

## 2. **Morale** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /məˈrɑːl/
**Definition:** The confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 士氣 (si6 hei3)

**Example 1:**
The team's morale was low after the series of defeats.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
喺一連串嘅失敗之後，隊伍嘅士氣好低落。
(hai2 jat1 lin4 cyun3 ge3 sat1 baai6 zi1 hau6, deoi2 ng5 ge3 si6 hei3 hou2 dai1 lok6.)

**Example 2:**
A good leader knows how to boost morale within the company.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
一個好嘅領袖知道點樣提高公司內部嘅士氣。
(jat1 go3 hou2 ge3 ling5 siu6 zi1 dou3 dim2 joeng2 tai4 gou1 gung1 si1 noi6 bou6 ge3 si6 hei3.)

## 3. **Survivors** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /səˈvaɪvərz/
**Definition:** People who have lived through a dangerous event or ordeal.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 生還者 (saang1 waan4 ze2)

**Example 1:**
The survivors of the earthquake were provided with shelter and food.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
地震嘅生還者被提供咗庇護所同食物。
(dei6 zan3 ge3 saang1 waan4 ze2 bei2 cung4 gung1 zo2 bei3 wu6 so2 tung4 sik6 mat6.)

**Example 2:**
The film tells the stories of the survivors of the Holocaust.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
套電影講述咗大屠殺嘅生還者嘅故事。
(tou3 din6 jing2 gong2 seot6 zo2 daai6 tou4 saat3 ge3 saang1 waan4 ze2 ge3 gu3 si6.)

## 4. **Explode** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪkˈsploʊd/
**Definition:** To burst or shatter violently and noisily as a result of rapid combustion, decomposition, or impact.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 爆炸 (baau3 zaa3)

**Example 1:**
The bomb exploded with a deafening roar.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰個炸彈喺一聲震耳欲聾嘅巨響中爆炸。
(go2 go3 zaa3 daan2 hai2 jat1 seng1 zan3 ji5 juk6 lung4 ge3 geoi6 hoeng2 zung1 baau3 zaa3.)

**Example 2:**
Shares in the company exploded after the announcement of the new product.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺新產品宣佈之後，公司嘅股份價格暴漲。
(hai2 san1 caan2 ban2 syun1 bou3 zi1 hau6, gung1 si1 ge3 gu2 fan2 gaa3 gaak3 bou6 zoeng2.)

## 5. **Asylum seekers** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /əˈsaɪləm ˈsiːkərz/
**Definition:** People who have left their country and are seeking protection from persecution in another country.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 尋求庇護者 (cam4 kau4 bei3 wu6 ze2)

**Example 1:**
Many asylum seekers arrive in the UK seeking a better life.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
好多尋求庇護者嚟到英國，希望可以得到更好嘅生活。
(hou2 do1 cam4 kau4 bei3 wu6 ze2 lai4 dou3 jing1 gwok3, hei1 mong6 ho2 ji5 dak1 dou3 gang3 hou2 ge3 sang1 wut6.)

**Example 2:**
The government is struggling to process the applications of asylum seekers.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
政府正努力處理尋求庇護者嘅申請。
(zing3 fu2 zing3 nou5 lik6 cyu2 lei5 cam4 kau4 bei3 wu6 ze2 ge3 san1 cing2.)

## 6. **Target** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtɑːrɡɪt/
**Definition:** To select as an object of attack or special attention.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 瞄準 (miu4 zeon2) / 針對 (zaam1 deoi3)

**Example 1:**
The company is targeting young professionals with its new advertising campaign.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
公司正針對年輕專業人士推出新嘅廣告宣傳活動。
(gung1 si1 zing3 zaam1 deoi3 nin4 ging1 zyun1 jip6 jan4 si6 tui1 ceot1 san1 ge3 gwong2 gou3 syun1 cyun4 wut6 dung6.)

**Example 2:**
The missile targeted the enemy's military base.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個導彈瞄準咗敵人嘅軍事基地。
(go2 go3 dou6 daan2 miu4 zeon2 zo2 dik6 jan4 ge3 gwan1 si6 gei1 dei2.)
