# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-10

Hello everyone!

## 1. **Chuffed** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃʌft/
**Definition:** Very pleased or delighted.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好開心, 好滿意

**Example 1:**
I was absolutely chuffed to win the competition.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我贏咗比賽，真係好開心。

**Example 2:**
She was chuffed with her exam results.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢對佢嘅考試成績好滿意。

## 2. **Knackered** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnækəd/
**Definition:** Extremely tired; exhausted.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好攰, 殘哂

**Example 1:**
After the hike, I was absolutely knackered.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
行完山之後，我真係殘哂。

**Example 2:**
I'm too knackered to go out tonight.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我太攰，今晚唔想出去。

## 3. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒi/
**Definition:** Suspicious, unreliable, or potentially dangerous.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑嘅，唔可靠嘅，有危險嘅

**Example 1:**
That car looks a bit dodgy to me.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰架車睇落有啲可疑。

**Example 2:**
I got some dodgy advice from a stranger.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我從一個陌生人度攞到啲唔可靠嘅建議。

## 4. **Cheeky** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtʃiːki/
**Definition:** Impudent, disrespectful, or playfully rude.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 厚面皮嘅，無禮貌嘅，頑皮嘅

**Example 1:**
He gave me a cheeky grin.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢對我露齒而笑，笑容有啲頑皮。

**Example 2:**
Don't be so cheeky to your elders!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
唔好對你嘅長輩咁無禮貌！

## 5. **Sorted** (Adjective/Verb - past participle)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsɔːtɪd/
**Definition:** Organised or dealt with successfully; resolved. Also past participle of the verb "to sort."
**Cantonese Meaning:** 搞掂咗，解決咗，整理好咗

**Example 1:**
The problem is finally sorted.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個問題終於搞掂咗。

**Example 2:**
Everything is sorted for the trip; we're ready to go.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
所有嘢都準備好哂，我哋可以出發喇。

## 6. **Quid** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /kwɪd/
**Definition:** A pound sterling (British currency).
**Cantonese Meaning:** 鎊 (英鎊)

**Example 1:**
That will cost you five quid.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰個會用你五鎊。

**Example 2:**
I only have a few quid in my pocket.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我得幾鎊喺我袋入面。

## 7. **Taking the Mickey** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkɪŋ ðə ˈmɪki/
**Definition:** To make fun of someone, tease, or ridicule.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 攞人哋嚟玩，取笑

**Example 1:**
Are you taking the Mickey out of me?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你係咪攞我嚟玩？

**Example 2:**
He's always taking the Mickey out of my accent.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢成日取笑我嘅口音。
