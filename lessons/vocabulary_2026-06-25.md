# Daily English Lesson - 2026-06-25

## 1. **Aftermath** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɑːftəmæθ/
**Definition:** The period of time after a destructive event or disaster.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 後果，餘波 (通常指災難後)

**Example 1:**
The city began the long process of rebuilding in the aftermath of the earthquake.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
地震過後，這座城市開始了漫長的重建過程。

**Example 2:**
Reporters arrived on the scene to cover the aftermath of the explosion.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
記者抵達現場報導爆炸後的餘波。

## 2. **Bruising** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbruːzɪŋ/
**Definition:** Very difficult or painful; causing emotional or physical hardship.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 艱難的，令人受挫的

**Example 1:**
The team suffered a bruising defeat in the final match of the tournament.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
球隊在錦標賽的決賽中遭遇了一場令人受挫的慘敗。

**Example 2:**
It has been a bruising week for the government as the new policy faces widespread criticism.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
這對政府來說是艱難的一週，因為這項新政策面臨廣泛批評。

## 3. **Soared** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /sɔːd/
**Definition:** To increase quickly to a high level.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 急升，飆升

**Example 1:**
Temperatures soared across the country, reaching record highs yesterday.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
全國氣溫飆升，昨日達到紀錄高位。

**Example 2:**
Energy prices have soared, causing concern for many low-income families.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
能源價格急升，令許多低收入家庭感到擔憂。

## 4. **Grappling** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡræplɪŋ/
**Definition:** To struggle to deal with or overcome a difficult situation.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 努力克服，掙扎應對

**Example 1:**
Millions of people are grappling with the intense heatwave affecting the continent.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
數百萬人正努力應對影響整個大陸的強烈熱浪。

**Example 2:**
The company is currently grappling with significant technical problems in its new software.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
這家公司目前正努力應對其新軟件中的重大技術問題。

## 5. **Rebuked** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /rɪˈbjuːkt/
**Definition:** To express sharp disapproval or criticism of someone's actions.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 指責，斥責

**Example 1:**
Lawmakers officially rebuked the leader for his sudden decision to start the military operation.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
立法者正式指責領導人突然決定開展軍事行動。

**Example 2:**
She was rebuked by her manager for failing to meet the project deadline.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
她因為未能趕上項目截止日期而受到經理的斥責。

## 6. **Scepticism** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈskeptɪsɪzəm/
**Definition:** An attitude of doubt as to the truth of something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 懷疑態度

**Example 1:**
Many experts expressed scepticism regarding the accuracy of the new scientific data.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
許多專家對新科學數據的準確性表示懷疑。

**Example 2:**
There is a lot of public scepticism about whether the plan will actually succeed.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
公眾對於該計劃是否真的會成功存有許多懷疑。
