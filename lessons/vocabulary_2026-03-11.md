# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-11

Hi there, today we'll be learning some new words and phrases!

## 1. **Cheeky** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtʃiːki/
**Definition:** Impudent, disrespectful in a playful or amusing way.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 厚面皮 (hau5 min6 pei4) / 曳 (jai6)

**Example 1:**
That's a rather cheeky remark to make to your supervisor!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
同你上司講呢啲嘢都幾厚面皮喎！
(tung4 nei5 soeng6 si1 gong2 ni1 di1 je5 dou1 gei2 hau5 min6 pei4 wo3!)

**Example 2:**
He gave me a cheeky grin.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢對住我古惑咁笑。
(keoi5 deoi3 zyu6 ngo5 gu2 waak6 gam2 siu3.)

## 2. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed or upset.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望 (hou2 si1 mong6) / 頹 (teoi4)

**Example 1:**
I was absolutely gutted when I failed the exam.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我考試唔合格，真係好失望。
(ngo5 haau2 si5 m4 hap6 gaak3, zan1 hai6 hou2 si1 mong6.)

**Example 2:**
She was gutted to hear about her friend's accident.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢聽到佢朋友嘅意外覺得好唔開心。
(keoi5 ting1 dou2 keoi5 pang4 jau5 ge3 ji3 ngoi6 gok3 dak1 hou2 m4 hoi1 sam1.)

## 3. **Chuffed** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃʌft/
**Definition:** Very pleased or satisfied.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好開心 (hou2 hoi1 sam1) / 好滿意 (hou2 mun5 ji3)

**Example 1:**
I'm really chuffed with my new car.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我對架新車真係好滿意。
(ngo5 deoi3 gaa3 san1 ce1 zan1 hai6 hou2 mun5 ji3.)

**Example 2:**
She was chuffed to win the competition.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢贏咗比賽，好開心。
(keoi5 wing4 zo2 bei2 coi3, hou2 hoi1 sam1.)

## 4. **Taking the Mickey (out of someone)** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkɪŋ ðə ˈmɪki/
**Definition:** To tease or make fun of someone in a playful way.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 玩嘢 (waan2 je5) / 攞嚟搞 (lo2 lai4 gaau2)

**Example 1:**
Are you taking the Mickey out of me?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你係咪玩嘢呀？
(nei5 hai6 mai1 waan2 je5 aa3?)

**Example 2:**
Stop taking the Mickey out of him, it's not funny.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
唔好再玩佢啦，唔好笑嘅。
(m4 hou2 zoi3 waan2 keoi5 laa1, m4 hou2 siu3 ge3.)

## 5. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒi/
**Definition:** Suspicious, unreliable, or potentially dangerous.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑 (ho2 ji4) / 唔安全 (m4 on1 cyun4)

**Example 1:**
That area of town is a bit dodgy at night.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰頭夜晚有啲唔安全。
(go2 tau4 je6 maan1 jau5 di1 m4 on1 cyun4.)

**Example 2:**
I bought a dodgy watch from a street vendor.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我喺街邊買咗隻可疑嘅錶。
(ngo5 hai2 gaai1 bin1 maai5 zo2 zek3 ho2 ji4 ge3 biu1.)

## 6. **Sorted** (Adjective/Verb - past participle)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsɔːtɪd/
**Definition:** Organised, arranged, or dealt with successfully.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 搞掂 (gaau2 dim6) / 搞好咗 (gaau2 hou2 zo2)

**Example 1:**
I've got the tickets sorted for the concert.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我已經搞掂咗演唱會嘅飛。
(ngo5 ji5 ging1 gaau2 dim6 zo2 jin2 coeng3 wui2 ge3 fei1.)

**Example 2:**
Everything's sorted now, so we can relax.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
所有嘢都搞掂晒，我哋可以放鬆吓。
(so2 jau5 je5 dou1 gaau2 dim6 saai3, ngo5 dei6 ho2 ji5 fong3 sung1 haa5.)
