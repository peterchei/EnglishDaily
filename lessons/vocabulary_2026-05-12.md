# Daily English Lesson - 2026-05-12

Hi there! Here are some useful words and phrases from today's news:

## 1. **Hang on by a thread** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation:** /hæŋ ɒn baɪ ə θrɛd/
**Definition:** To be in a very precarious or unstable situation; close to failure.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 岌岌可危 (gap gap ho nguy)

**Example 1:**
The Prime Minister is hanging on by a thread after the latest scandal.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
喺最新嘅醜聞之後，首相岌岌可危。
(Hai jui san ge chau man ji hau, sau seung gap gap ho nguy.)

**Example 2:**
The company is hanging on by a thread due to the economic downturn.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
由於經濟衰退，公司岌岌可危。
(Yau yu ging jai sui tui, gung si gap gap ho nguy.)

## 2. **Unprecedented** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ʌnˈpresɪdentɪd/
**Definition:** Never done or known before.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 前所未有 (chin so mei yau)

**Example 1:**
The attack was an unprecedented act of aggression.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
呢次襲擊係前所未有嘅侵略行為。
(Ni chi chap gik hai chin so mei yau ge chim luk hang wai.)

**Example 2:**
The company experienced unprecedented growth this year.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
今年公司經歷咗前所未有嘅增長。
(Gam nin gung si ging lik jo chin so mei yau ge jang jeung.)

## 3. **Arbitrary** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɑːrbɪtrəri/
**Definition:** Based on random choice or personal whim, rather than any reason or system.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 任意嘅 (yam yi ge)

**Example 1:**
The judge made an arbitrary decision.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰位法官做咗一個任意嘅決定。
(Go wai fat guan jo jo yat go yam yi ge guet ding.)

**Example 2:**
The rules seemed arbitrary and unfair.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
啲規則睇落任意而且唔公平。
(Di gwai zak tai lok yam yi yi che mut gung ping.)

## 4. **Whirlwind** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈwɜːrlwɪnd/
**Definition:** A situation or event in which a lot of things happen very quickly.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 旋風式 (suen fung sik)

**Example 1:**
His visit was a whirlwind of meetings and events.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢嘅訪問係旋風式嘅會議同活動。
(Keui ge fong mun hai suen fung sik ge wui yi tung wut dung.)

**Example 2:**
The wedding was a whirlwind of excitement.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個婚禮係旋風式嘅興奮。
(Go go fan lai hai suen fung sik ge hing fun.)

## 5. **Bear fruit** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation:** /beər fruːt/
**Definition:** To produce good results; to be successful.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 開花結果 (hoi fa git gwo)

**Example 1:**
Hopefully, the negotiations will bear fruit.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
希望啲談判會開花結果。
(Hei mong di taam pun wui hoi fa git gwo.)

**Example 2:**
His hard work eventually bore fruit.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢嘅努力最終開花結果。
(Keui ge nu lik jui chung hoi fa git gwo.)

## 6. **Subscription** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /səbˈskrɪpʃən/
**Definition:** An agreement to receive a publication, service, or product regularly by paying in advance.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 訂閱 (ding yuet)

**Example 1:**
I have a subscription to a magazine.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我訂閱咗一份雜誌。
(Ngo ding yuet jo yat fan jaap ji.)

**Example 2:**
The streaming service offers different subscription plans.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
個串流服務提供唔同嘅訂閱計劃。
(Go chuen lau fuk mouk tai cung mut tung ge ding yuet gai waak.)

## 7. **Council tax** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈkaʊnsəl tæks/
**Definition:** A tax paid by people who live in a particular local authority area to help pay for services such as schools and rubbish collection.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 市政稅 (si jing shui)

**Example 1:**
You have to pay council tax if you own or rent a property.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
如果你擁有或租用物業，你就必須繳納市政稅。
(Yu guo nei yung yau waak jui yung mat ye, nei jau bit sui giu na si jing shui.)

**Example 2:**
The council tax bill has increased this year.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
今年嘅市政稅單增加咗。
(Gam nin ge si jing shui daan jang ga jo.)
