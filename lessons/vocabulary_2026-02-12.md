# Daily Vocabulary: Workplace Essentials

1. **Touch base** (Idiom)
**Meaning**: To contact someone briefly to update them or get an update.
**Cantonese**: 聯絡一下 / 跟進 (Luen lok yat ha / Gan jun)
**Pronunciation**: /tʌtʃ beɪs/
**Example**: I'll touch base with you next week regarding the project status.
**Translation**: 我下星期會同你聯絡一下關於個項目嘅進度。

2. **Heads up** (Noun / Phrase)
**Meaning**: An advance warning or notification.
**Cantonese**: 提提你 / 預先通知 (Tai tai nei / Yue sin tung ji)
**Pronunciation**: /hɛdz ʌp/
**Example**: Just a heads up, the meeting has been moved to 2 PM.
**Translation**: 提提你，個會改咗去下午兩點。

3. **Loop in** (Phrasal Verb)
**Meaning**: To keep someone informed or involved in a discussion.
**Cantonese**: 通知埋 / CC埋 (Tung ji maai / CC maai)
**Pronunciation**: /luːp ɪn/
**Example**: Please loop me in on any emails regarding the client.
**Translation**: 所有關於個客嘅電郵麻煩都通知埋我。

4. **Call it a day** (Idiom)
**Meaning**: To stop working on something, usually because you have done enough or are tired.
**Cantonese**: 收工 / 到此為止 (Sau gung / Dou chi wai ji)
**Pronunciation**: /kɔːl ɪt ə deɪ/
**Example**: We've done a lot today, let's call it a day.
**Translation**: 我哋今日做咗好多嘢喇，收工啦。
