# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-18

Hi there! Today's lesson will focus on some useful words and phrases for your British English.

## 1. **Cheers** (Exclamation/Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** An informal expression used to mean "thank you", "goodbye", or as a toast before drinking.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 多謝, 再見, 乾杯

**Example 1:**
Cheers for helping me with my homework!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
多謝你幫我做功課呀！(Do je nei bong ngo jo gung fo a!)

**Example 2:**
Cheers, I'm off now. See you later!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
再見，我走先喇。遲啲見！(Joi gin, ngo jau sin la. Chi di gin!)

## 2. **Queue** (Noun/Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /kjuː/
**Definition:** A line of people waiting for something; to stand in a line.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 隊伍; 排隊

**Example 1:**
There's a long queue at the post office today.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今日郵局有條好長嘅隊。 (Gam yat yau guk yau tiu ho cheung ge dui.)

**Example 2:**
You need to queue up if you want to get in.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
你想入去嘅話，就要排隊。 (Nei seung yap hui ge wa, jau yiu pai dui.)

## 3. **Lovely** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈlʌv.li/
**Definition:** Beautiful; very pleasing or enjoyable.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可愛, 靚, 好

**Example 1:**
What a lovely day for a picnic!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今日天氣咁好，好啱去野餐呀！ (Gam yat tin hei gam ho, ho ngaam hui ye chan a!)

**Example 2:**
She has a lovely smile.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢有個好可愛嘅笑容。 (Kui yau go ho hoh oi ge siu yung.)

## 4. **Brilliant** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbrɪl.i.ənt/
**Definition:** Very intelligent or talented; very good.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 聰明, 好棒, 精彩

**Example 1:**
She's a brilliant scientist.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢係一個好聰明嘅科學家。 (Kui hai yat go ho chung ming ge fo hok ga.)

**Example 2:**
That's a brilliant idea!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢個主意真係好棒！ (Ni go jue yi jan hai ho bang!)

## 5. **Knackered** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnæk.əd/
**Definition:** Very tired; exhausted. (Informal British English)
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好攰

**Example 1:**
I'm absolutely knackered after that run.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
跑完步之後，我真係好攰呀。 (Pau yun bou ji hau, ngo jan hai ho gui a.)

**Example 2:**
He was knackered after working all day.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢做咗成日嘢之後，好攰。 (Kui jo jor seng yat ye ji hau, ho gui.)

## 6. **To fancy** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfæn.si/
**Definition:** To want something or want to do something; to be attracted to someone.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 想要; 鍾意

**Example 1:**
Do you fancy going to the cinema tonight?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你今晚想唔想去睇戲啊？ (Nei gam maan seung m seung hui tai hei a?)

**Example 2:**
I think he fancies her.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我覺得佢鍾意佢。(Ngo gok dak kui chung yi kui.)

## 7. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒ.i/
**Definition:** Suspicious or unreliable; potentially dangerous or illegal. (Informal British English)
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑, 危險

**Example 1:**
That restaurant looks a bit dodgy.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰間餐廳睇落有啲可疑。 (Go gaan chaan teng tai lok yau di ho yi.)

**Example 2:**
I got some dodgy advice from him.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我從佢嗰度攞到啲唔可靠嘅建議。(Ngo chung kui go do lo dou di m ho kao ge gin yi.)
