# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-07

## 1. **Ultimatum** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌʌltɪˈmeɪtəm/
**Definition:** A final demand or statement of terms, the rejection of which will result in retaliation or a breakdown in relations.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 最後通牒 (zeoi3 hau6 tung1 dip3)

**Example 1:**
The president issued an ultimatum to the opposing country, demanding immediate compliance.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
總統向敵對國家發出最後通牒，要求立即服從。
(Zung2 tung2 hoeng3 dik6 deoi3 gwok3 faat3 ceot1 zeoi3 hau6 tung1 dip3, jiu3 kau4 lik1 gik1 fuk6 cung4.)

**Example 2:**
She gave her son an ultimatum: either clean his room, or he wouldn't get to play video games.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢俾咗個最後通牒佢個仔：要唔係執房，要唔係就唔可以打機。
(Keoi5 bei2 zo2 go3 zeoi3 hau6 tung1 dip3 keoi5 go3 zai2: jiu3 m4 hai6 zap1 fong4, jiu3 m4 hai6 zau6 m4 ho2 ji5 daa2 gei1.)

## 2. **Strait** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /streɪt/
**Definition:** A narrow passage of water connecting two larger bodies of water.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 海峽 (hoi2 haap3)

**Example 1:**
The ship sailed through the Strait of Gibraltar.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
艘船駛過直布羅陀海峽。
(Sau1 syun4 sai2 gwo3 zik6 bou3 lo4 to4 hoi2 haap3.)

**Example 2:**
The Strait is a crucial trade route for many countries.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰條海峽係好多國家嘅重要貿易路線。
(Go2 tiu4 hoi2 haap3 hai6 ho2 do1 gwok3 gaa1 ge3 zung6 jiu3 mau6 jik6 lou6 sin3.)

## 3. **Disruption** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /dɪsˈrʌpʃən/
**Definition:** Disturbance or problems which interrupt an event, activity, or process.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 擾亂 (jiu5 lyun6) / 中斷 (zung1 dyun6)

**Example 1:**
The heavy snow caused major disruption to public transport.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
大雪對公共交通造成嚴重嘅擾亂。
(Daai6 syut3 deoi3 gung1 gung1 gaau1 tung1 zou6 sing4 jim4 zung6 ge3 jiu5 lyun6.)

**Example 2:**
The protestors caused significant disruption to the city centre.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
啲示威者對市中心造成嚴重嘅擾亂。
(Di1 si6 wai1 ze2 deoi3 si5 zung1 sam1 zou6 sing4 jim4 zung6 ge3 jiu5 lyun6.)

## 4. **Landmark** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈlændmɑːrk/
**Definition:** An event, discovery, or change marking an important stage or turning point in something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 具有里程碑意義嘅 (geoi6 jau5 lei5 cing4 bei1 ji3 ji6 ge3)

**Example 1:**
The court case was a landmark decision for environmental law.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰單官司係環境法嘅一個具有里程碑意義嘅決定。
(Go2 daan1 gun1 si1 hai6 waan4 ging2 faat3 ge3 jat1 go3 geoi6 jau5 lei5 cing4 bei1 ji3 ji6 ge3 kyut3 ding6.)

**Example 2:**
The discovery of penicillin was a landmark achievement in medicine.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
青霉素嘅發現係醫學上嘅一個具有里程碑意義嘅成就。
(Cing1 mui4 sou3 ge3 faat3 jin6 hai6 ji1 hok6 soeng6 ge3 jat1 go3 geoi6 jau5 lei5 cing4 bei1 ji3 ji6 ge3 sing4 zau6.)

## 5. **Alleged** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /əˈledʒd/
**Definition:** Said, without proof, to have taken place or to have a specified attribute.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 聲稱嘅 (seng1 caan1 ge3) / 涉嫌嘅 (sip3 jim4 ge3)

**Example 1:**
The alleged thief was arrested by the police.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個聲稱嘅賊人被警察拉咗。
(Go3 seng1 caan1 ge3 caak6 jan4 bei2 ging2 caat3 laai1 zo2.)

**Example 2:**
She denied the alleged affair.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢否認咗嗰單聲稱嘅婚外情。
(Keoi5 fau2 jing6 zo2 go2 daan1 seng1 caan1 ge3 fan1 ngoi6 cing4.)

## 6. **Hostile** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈhɒstaɪl/
**Definition:** Unfriendly; antagonistic.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 敵對嘅 (dik6 deoi3 ge3)

**Example 1:**
The soldiers entered hostile territory.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
啲士兵進入咗敵對嘅領土。
(Di1 si6 bing1 zeon3 jap6 zo2 dik6 deoi3 ge3 ling5 tou2.)

**Example 2:**
He received a very hostile reception at the meeting.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢喺個會議上面受到一個非常敵對嘅招待。
(Keoi5 hai2 go3 wui5 ji5 soeng6 min6 sau6 dou3 jat1 go3 fei1 soeng4 dik6 deoi3 ge3 ziu1 doi6.)

## 7. **Scramble** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈskræmbl/
**Definition:** To move somewhere quickly, often with difficulty, using your hands and feet.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 爭先恐後 (zang1 sin1 hung2 hau6) / 爬 (paa4)

**Example 1:**
People were scrambling to escape the burning building.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
啲人爭先恐後咁逃離嗰棟着火嘅建築物。
(Di1 jan4 zang1 sin1 hung2 hau6 gam2 tou4 lei4 go2 dung6 zoek3 fo2 ge3 gin3 zuk1 mat6.)

**Example 2:**
The climbers had to scramble up the steep rock face.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰啲攀山者要爬上陡峭嘅石壁。
(Go2 di1 paan1 saan1 ze2 jiu3 paa4 soeng5 dau2 ciu3 ge3 sek6 bik1.)
