# Daily English Lesson - 2026-06-30

Hi there! Today we are looking at some useful vocabulary derived from the latest headlines to help you sound more natural in British English.

## 1. **Negligence** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/
**Definition:** The failure to take proper care in doing something, resulting in damage or injury.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 疏忽；怠忽職守

**Example 1:**
The report concluded that the government’s negligence led to the delay in disaster relief.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
報告總結指政府的疏忽導致了救災工作的延誤。

**Example 2:**
He is being sued for professional negligence after the building project failed.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
建築項目失敗後，他因專業疏忽而被起訴。

## 2. **Apathy** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈæp.ə.θi/
**Definition:** A lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 冷漠；無動於衷

**Example 1:**
There is a growing sense of apathy among voters who feel their opinions no longer matter.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
選民對投票愈發感到冷漠，認為自己的意見已無關緊要。

**Example 2:**
The director was criticized for his apathy towards the staff's poor working conditions.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
該主管因對員工惡劣的工作環境表現冷漠而受到批評。

## 3. **Overhaul** (Noun/Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈəʊ.və.hɔːl/
**Definition:** To repair or improve something, especially a system or machine, so that every part of it works as it should.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 全面檢修；徹底改革

**Example 1:**
The maternity services require a major overhaul to ensure patient safety.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
產科服務需要進行徹底改革，以確保病人的安全。

**Example 2:**
We are planning to overhaul our internal IT systems by the end of the year.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我們計劃在年底前對內部資訊科技系統進行全面檢修。

## 4. **Fend for oneself** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /fend fɔː wʌnˈself/
**Definition:** To take care of oneself without help from anyone else.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 獨自應付；自力更生

**Example 1:**
After the earthquake, many families were left to fend for themselves while waiting for aid.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
地震發生後，許多家庭在等待援助的同時，不得不獨自應付生活。

**Example 2:**
Moving to a new city at eighteen forced me to learn how to fend for myself.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
十八歲搬到新城市生活，迫使我學習如何自力更生。

## 5. **Custody** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈkʌs.tə.di/
**Definition:** The legal right or duty to care for a child, especially after a divorce or separation.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 監護權；拘留

**Example 1:**
The parents are currently engaged in a bitter legal battle over the custody of their daughter.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
這對父母目前正為女兒的監護權進行激烈的法律鬥爭。

**Example 2:**
The suspect was taken into police custody shortly after the incident.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
事件發生後不久，嫌疑人便被警方拘留。

## 6. **Setback** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈset.bæk/
**Definition:** A problem that makes progress more difficult or slows it down.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 挫折；阻礙

**Example 1:**
The latest court ruling is a significant setback for the president’s new policy.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
最新的法庭裁決對總統的新政策而言是一個重大的挫折。

**Example 2:**
Despite a few minor setbacks, the project was completed on schedule.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
儘管遇到一些小挫折，該項目還是按計劃完成了。

## 7. **Veteran** (Adjective/Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈvet.ər.ən/
**Definition:** Someone who has a lot of experience in a particular activity or job.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 資深的；經驗豐富的

**Example 1:**
The company decided to hire veteran engineers to oversee the quality control process.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
公司決定聘請資深工程師來監督品質控制流程。

**Example 2:**
She is a veteran journalist who has reported from all over the world.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
她是一位經驗豐富的記者，曾從世界各地進行報導。
