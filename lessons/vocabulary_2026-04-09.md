# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-09

## N. **Respite** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈrɛspaɪt/
**Definition:** A short period of rest or relief from something difficult or unpleasant.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 喘息 (chuen2 sik1), 休息 (yau1 sik1)

**Example 1:**
The ceasefire provided a brief respite for the war-torn city.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
停火為飽受戰爭蹂躪的城市提供了一個短暫的喘息機會。

**Example 2:**
She needed a respite from caring for her elderly mother.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
她需要從照顧年邁的母親中得到休息。

## N. **Frank** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /fræŋk/
**Definition:** Open, honest, and direct in speech or writing, especially when dealing with unwelcome matters.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 坦率 (taan2 seut3), 真誠 (zan1 sing4)

**Example 1:**
Their discussion was very frank and addressed all the difficult issues.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
他們的討論非常坦率，並解決了所有困難的問題。

**Example 2:**
To be frank, I don't think your plan will work.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
坦率地說，我不認為你的計劃會成功。

## N. **Variant** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈveəriənt/
**Definition:** A form or version of something that differs in some respect from other forms of the same thing or from a standard.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 變體 (bin3 tai2), 版本 (baan2 bun2)

**Example 1:**
This new variant of the software is much faster.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
這個軟件的新版本快得多。

**Example 2:**
There are several variants of the traditional recipe.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
傳統食譜有幾個變體。

## N. **Provisional** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /prəˈvɪʒənəl/
**Definition:** Arranged or existing for the present, possibly to be changed later.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 臨時 (lam4 si4), 暫定 (zaam6 ding6)

**Example 1:**
The ceasefire agreement is only provisional and needs further negotiation.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
停火協議只是臨時的，需要進一步談判。

**Example 2:**
We have made a provisional booking for the restaurant.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我們已經為餐廳做了臨時預訂。

## N. **Unprecedented** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ʌnˈprɛsɪˌdɛntɪd/
**Definition:** Never done or known before.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 前所未有 (chin4 so2 mei6 yau5), 史無前例 (si2 mou4 chin4 lai6)

**Example 1:**
The storm caused an unprecedented level of damage.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
這場風暴造成了前所未有的破壞。

**Example 2:**
This is an unprecedented opportunity to invest in the company.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
這是一個投資該公司的前所未有的機會。

## N. **Elusive** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪˈluːsɪv/
**Definition:** Difficult to find, catch, or achieve.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 難以捉摸 (naan4 yi5 zuk1 mo1), 逃避 (tou4 bei6)

**Example 1:**
The solution to the problem remained elusive.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
這個問題的解決方案仍然難以捉摸。

**Example 2:**
He is an elusive character who rarely gives interviews.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
他是一個難以捉摸的人，很少接受採訪。

## N. **Coordinated** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪtɪd/
**Definition:** Brought into a common action, movement, or condition; concerted.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 協調 (hip3 tiu4), 配合 (pui3 hap6)

**Example 1:**
The rescue operation was a coordinated effort between several agencies.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
救援行動是幾個機構之間協調一致的努力。

**Example 2:**
They launched a coordinated attack on the enemy forces.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
他們對敵軍發動了協調一致的攻擊。
