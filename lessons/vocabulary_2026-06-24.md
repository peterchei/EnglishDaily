# Daily English Lesson - 2026-06-24

## 1. **Drab** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /dræb/
**Definition:** Lacking brightness or interest; drearily dull in appearance or character.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 單調的，沉悶的

**Example 1:**
The team’s drab performance left the supporters feeling very disappointed.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
球隊單調的表現令支持者感到非常失望。

**Example 2:**
It was a drab, grey afternoon, perfect for staying inside.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
那是一個沉悶、灰暗的下午，最適合待在室內。

## 2. **Passive** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈpæs.ɪv/
**Definition:** Accepting or allowing what happens or what others do, without active response or resistance.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 被動的，消極的

**Example 1:**
The coach was frustrated because the players were too passive during the first half.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
教練感到很沮喪，因為球員在上半場表現得太被動了。

**Example 2:**
He prefers a passive role in group projects, letting others lead the way.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
他在小組項目中傾向於採取被動角色，讓其他人去主導。

## 3. **Lament** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ləˈment/
**Definition:** To express passionate grief about or regret for something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 哀嘆，痛惜

**Example 1:**
Fans continue to lament the missed opportunities during the tournament.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
球迷們繼續為比賽中錯失的機會感到痛惜。

**Example 2:**
She lamented the loss of the historic building in her local area.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
她對她居住地區那座歷史建築的拆除感到哀嘆。

## 4. **Unseat** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ʌnˈsiːt/
**Definition:** To remove someone from a position of power or a job, especially by defeating them in an election.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 把……趕下台，（在選舉中）擊敗

**Example 1:**
The challenger managed to unseat the incumbent after a long and difficult campaign.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
經過長期且艱難的競選後，挑戰者成功將現任者趕下台。

**Example 2:**
Public anger over the economy helped to unseat the government representative.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
公眾對經濟的不滿有助於讓政府代表落選。

## 5. **Amputate** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈæm.pjə.teɪt/
**Definition:** To cut off a limb by surgical operation.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 截肢

**Example 1:**
Doctors had to amputate his leg following the severe infection.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
醫生在嚴重感染後不得不為他截肢。

**Example 2:**
The surgeons performed the procedure to amputate the damaged tissue and save her life.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
外科醫生執行了手術，截除了受損組織以挽救她的生命。

## 6. **Plaintiff** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈpleɪn.tɪf/
**Definition:** A person who brings a case against another in a court of law.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 原告

**Example 1:**
The plaintiff argued that the company had failed to protect user privacy.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
原告辯稱該公司未能保護用戶隱私。

**Example 2:**
Both the plaintiff and the defendant were present at the hearing today.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
原告和被告今天都出席了聽證會。

## 7. **Buckle** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbʌk.əl/
**Definition:** To bend and give way under pressure or heat; to collapse.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 彎曲，屈服，崩潰

**Example 1:**
The infrastructure began to buckle under the intense heat of the record-breaking summer.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
在破紀錄夏季的酷熱下，基礎設施開始變形彎曲。

**Example 2:**
The pressure was so high that the team finally started to buckle.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
壓力太大了，團隊最終開始承受不住而崩潰。
