# Daily English Lesson - 2026-05-15

Hello everyone! Today, we'll learn some new words from today's news.

## 1. **Mounts** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /maʊnts/
**Definition:** To increase or grow gradually.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 增加; 增長

**Example 1:**
Pressure mounts on the Prime Minister to resign.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
首相辭職嘅壓力越來越大。
(So seung chi jik ge aat lik yut loi yut daai.)

**Example 2:**
The evidence mounts against the suspect.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
指控嫌疑人嘅證據越來越多。
(Ji gung him yi yan ge jing gui yut loi yut do.)

## 2. **Vigilante** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌvɪdʒɪˈlænti/
**Definition:** A person who takes the law into their own hands, without legal authority.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 治安維持者; 私刑者

**Example 1:**
The town was terrorized by vigilante groups.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個鎮被治安維持者團體恐嚇。
(Go zan bei ji on wai chi je tuen tai hung haak.)

**Example 2:**
He became a vigilante after the police failed to solve the crime.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
警察未能破案後，佢成為咗一個私刑者。
(Ging chaat mei nang po on hau, kui sing wai jo yat go si ying je.)

## 3. **Transparency** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /trænsˈpærənsi/
**Definition:** The quality of being open and honest; free from secrecy.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 透明度

**Example 1:**
The public is demanding greater transparency in government spending.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
公眾要求政府開支有更高嘅透明度。
(Gung jung yiu kau jing fu hoi ji yau gang go ge tau ming dou.)

**Example 2:**
We need more transparency in the company's financial dealings.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我哋需要公司財務交易有更高嘅透明度。
(Ngo dei sui yiu gung si choi mo jiao yik yau gang go ge tau ming dou.)

## 4. **Designated** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdezɪɡneɪtɪd/
**Definition:** Officially named or chosen for a specific purpose.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 指定嘅

**Example 1:**
This area is a designated bathing site.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
呢個區域係一個指定嘅泳灘。
(Ni go kui wik hai yat go ji ding ge wing taan.)

**Example 2:**
He is the designated driver for tonight.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢係今晚嘅指定司機。
(Kui hai gam maan ge ji ding si gei.)

## 5. **Fanfare** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfænfeər/
**Definition:** A showy public display or celebration.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 誇耀; 炫耀

**Example 1:**
The new product was launched with great fanfare.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
新產品在盛大嘅儀式中推出。
(San chan ban joi sing daai ge yi sik jung tui chui.)

**Example 2:**
There was a lot of fanfare surrounding the president's visit.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
總統嘅訪問受到咗好多嘅誇耀。
(Zung tung ge fong man sau dou jo ho do ge kwa yiu.)

## 6. **Seized** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /siːzd/
**Definition:** To take hold of suddenly and forcibly.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 奪取; 扣押

**Example 1:**
The ship was seized by pirates.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
艘船被海盜奪取咗。
(Sau suen bei hoi dou duet chui jo.)

**Example 2:**
Customs officers seized the illegal goods.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
海關人員扣押咗嗰啲非法貨物。
(Hoi gwaan yan yun kau aap jo go di fei faat fo mat.)

## 7. **Rubble** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈrʌbəl/
**Definition:** Broken pieces of stone, brick, or other material, as from a demolished building.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 瓦礫; 碎石

**Example 1:**
The survivors were pulled from the rubble.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
生還者被從瓦礫中救出嚟。
(Saang waan je bei chung ngaa lik jung gau chui lai.)

**Example 2:**
The earthquake reduced the city to rubble.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
地震將嗰個城市變成咗瓦礫。
(Dei zan jeung go go sing si bin sing jo ngaa lik.)
