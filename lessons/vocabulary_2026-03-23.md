# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-23

## 1. **Queue** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /kjuː/
**Definition:** A line of people or vehicles waiting for something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 隊伍 (deoi2 ng5)

**Example 1:**
There's a long queue outside the bakery this morning.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今日朝早喺麵包舖出邊有條好長嘅隊。

**Example 2:**
Please join the queue if you want to buy a ticket.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
如果你想買飛，請排隊。

## 2. **Cheers** (Exclamation)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** A toast of goodwill or gratitude, often used when drinking; also a casual way of saying "thank you" or "goodbye".
**Cantonese Meaning:** 乾杯 (gon1 bui1); 多謝 (do1 ze6); 再見 (zoi3 gin3)

**Example 1:**
Cheers! To your success in the new job!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
乾杯! 祝你喺新工作上成功!

**Example 2:**
"Here's your coffee." "Cheers!"
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"呢杯係你嘅咖啡。" "多謝!"

## 3. **Rubbish** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈrʌbɪʃ/
**Definition:** Waste material; trash. Also, something of poor quality or worthless.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 垃圾 (laap6 saap3); 廢話 (fai3 waa6)

**Example 1:**
Please put the rubbish in the bin.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
請將垃圾放入垃圾桶。

**Example 2:**
That movie was complete rubbish!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰套戲真係完全係廢嘢！

## 4. **Brilliant** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbrɪliənt/
**Definition:** Exceptionally clever or talented; very bright and shining. Also, excellent or wonderful.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 聰明 (cung1 ming4); 光明 (gwong1 ming4); 好棒 (hou2 paang5)

**Example 1:**
She gave a brilliant performance in the play.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢喺戲劇入面嘅演出好棒。

**Example 2:**
That's a brilliant idea!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個係個好聰明嘅主意！

## 5. **Fancy** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfænsi/
**Definition:** To want something or want to do something; to find someone attractive.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 想要 (soeng2 jiu3); 鍾意 (zung1 ji3)

**Example 1:**
Do you fancy going to the cinema tonight?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你今晚想唔想去睇戲呀？

**Example 2:**
I think he fancies her.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我覺得佢鍾意佢。

## 6. **Takeaway** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkəweɪ/
**Definition:** A restaurant or shop that sells cooked food to be eaten elsewhere; also, a key point or message learned from something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 外賣 (ngoi6 maai6); 重點 (zung6 dim2)

**Example 1:**
Let's get a takeaway for dinner tonight.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我哋今晚叫外賣做晚餐啦。

**Example 2:**
The main takeaway from the meeting was that we need to improve communication.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
會議嘅主要重點係我哋需要改善溝通。
