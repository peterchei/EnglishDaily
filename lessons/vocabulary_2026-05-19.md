# Daily English Lesson - 2026-05-19

## 1. **Fronting** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfrʌntɪŋ/
**Definition:** Presenting a particular image or appearance, often to conceal the truth.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 掩飾, 偽裝 (yim2 sik1, ngai6 zong1)

**Example 1:**
The shop was allegedly fronting a money-laundering operation.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
據稱，呢間鋪頭係掩飾緊一個洗黑錢嘅行動。
(geui3 cheng1, ne1 gaan1 pou3 tau4 hai6 yim2 sik1 gan2 jat1 go3 sai2 hak1 chin2 ge3 hang4 dung6.)

**Example 2:**
He was fronting as a legitimate businessman, but he was actually a drug dealer.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢偽裝成一個合法嘅商人，但係佢實際上係一個毒販。
(keui5 ngai6 zong1 sing4 jat1 go3 hap6 faat3 ge3 seung1 yan2, daan6 hai6 keui5 sat6 zeung6 hai6 jat1 go3 duk6 faan2.)

## 2. **Rhetoric** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈretərɪk/
**Definition:** The art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing, especially the use of figures of speech and other compositional techniques. Can also refer to empty or exaggerated speech.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 修辭 (sau1 chi4), 花言巧語 (faa1 yin4 haau2 yu5)

**Example 1:**
The politician's speech was full of empty rhetoric and promises he couldn't keep.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰位政客嘅演講充滿咗空洞嘅修辭同埋佢實現唔到嘅承諾。
(go2 wai2 jing3 hak1 ge3 yin2 gong2 chung1 mun5 zo2 hung1 dung6 ge3 sau1 chi4 tung4 mai4 keui5 sat6 hin6 m4 dou2 ge3 sing4 nok6.)

**Example 2:**
His rhetoric was very persuasive, even though I didn't agree with his policies.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢嘅修辭好有說服力，即使我唔同意佢嘅政策。
(keui5 ge3 sau1 chi4 hou2 jau5 suet3 fuk6 lik6, jik1 si2 ngo5 m4 tung1 yi3 keui5 ge3 jing3 chak1.)

## 3. **Nemesis** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈneməsɪs/
**Definition:** A long-standing enemy; an archenemy.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 死敵 (sei2 dik6), 宿敵 (suk1 dik6)

**Example 1:**
The superhero finally defeated his nemesis in an epic battle.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰位超級英雄喺一場史詩式嘅戰鬥中終於擊敗咗佢嘅死敵。
(go2 wai2 chiu1 kup1 ying1 hung4 hai2 jat1 cheung4 si2 si1 sik1 ge3 jin3 dau2 jung1 yu1 gei1 baai6 zo2 keui5 ge3 sei2 dik6.)

**Example 2:**
For years, the two companies had been each other's nemesis in the marketplace.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
幾年嚟，嗰兩間公司一直都係對方喺市場上嘅宿敵。
(gei2 nin4 lai4, go2 leung5 gaan1 gung1 si1 jat1 zik6 dou1 hai6 deui3 fong1 hai2 si5 cheung4 seung6 ge3 suk1 dik6.)

## 4. **Oust** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /aʊst/
**Definition:** To drive out or expel (someone) from a position or place.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 趕走 (gon2 zau2), 罷免 (baa6 min5)

**Example 1:**
The shareholders voted to oust the CEO from his position.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
股東投票決定將CEO趕走。
(gu2 dung1 tau4 piu3 kyut3 ding6 jeung1 CEO gon2 zau2.)

**Example 2:**
The rebels were trying to oust the government from power.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
叛軍試圖將政府趕出權力。
(bun6 gwan1 si3 tou4 jeung1 jing3 fu2 gon2 chut1 kuen4 lik6.)

## 5. **Fiscal** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfɪskəl/
**Definition:** Relating to government revenue, especially taxes.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 財政 (choi4 jing3)

**Example 1:**
The government needs to implement sound fiscal policies to reduce the national debt.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府需要實施健全嘅財政政策嚟減少國債。
(jing3 fu2 seui1 yiu3 sat6 si1 gin6 chuen4 ge3 choi4 jing3 jing3 chak1 lai4 gam2 siu2 gwok3 jaai3.)

**Example 2:**
The mayor's plan involved significant fiscal adjustments.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
市長嘅計劃涉及重大嘅財政調整。
(si5 cheung4 ge3 gai3 waak6 sip3 gap6 chung5 daai6 ge3 choi4 jing3 tiu4 jing2.)

## 6. **Staggering** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈstæɡərɪŋ/
**Definition:** So surprisingly impressive or great as to overwhelm.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 驚人嘅 (ging1 yan4 ge3), 令人難以置信嘅 (ling6 yan4 naan4 ji5 ji3 sun3 ge3)

**Example 1:**
The cost of the project was a staggering £2 billion.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰個項目嘅成本係一個令人難以置信嘅20億英鎊。
(go2 go3 hong6 muk6 ge3 sing4 bun2 hai6 jat1 go3 ling6 yan4 naan4 ji5 ji3 sun3 ge3 yi6 baak3 maan6 ying1 bong3.)

**Example 2:**
A staggering number of people are affected by the famine.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
受飢荒影響嘅人數多到令人震驚。
(sau6 gei1 fong1 ying2 heung2 ge3 yan4 sou3 do1 dou3 ling6 yan4 chan3 ging1.)

## 7. **Hedgehog** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈhɛdʒhɒɡ/
**Definition:** A small nocturnal Old World mammal with a body covered in sharp spines.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 刺蝟 (chi3 wai3)

**Example 1:**
We saw a hedgehog in the garden last night.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我哋琴晚喺花園見到一隻刺蝟。
(ngo5 dei6 kam4 maan5 hai2 faa1 yun4 gin3 dou2 jat1 jek3 chi3 wai3.)

**Example 2:**
Hedgehogs are becoming increasingly rare in the UK.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺英國，刺蝟變得越來越稀少。
(hai2 ying1 gwok3, chi3 wai3 bin3 dak1 yuet6 lai4 yuet6 hei1 siu2.)
