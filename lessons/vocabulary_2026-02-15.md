1. **Beat around the bush** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation**: /biːt əˈraʊnd ðə bʊʃ/
**Definition**: To avoid talking about what is important; to speak in an indirect way.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 講嘢轉彎抹角，唔到正題。
**Example**: Please stop beating around the bush and tell me what you really think.
**Cantonese Example**: 唔好再轉彎抹角喇，直接話我聽你真正諗法係點。

2. **In the loop** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation**: /ɪn ðə luːp/
**Definition**: To be kept informed about a specific situation or project.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 保持知情；喺圈子入面（知道進度）。
**Example**: I'll make sure to keep you in the loop regarding the upcoming project changes.
**Cantonese Example**: 我會確保你隨時知道嚟緊個項目嘅變動。

3. **Call it a day** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation**: /kɔːl ɪt ə deɪ/
**Definition**: To decide to stop working on something for the rest of the day.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 收工；今日做到呢度。
**Example**: We've been working for ten hours; let's call it a day.
**Cantonese Example**: 我哋已經做咗十個鐘喇，不如收工啦。

4. **Under the weather** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation**: /ˈʌndə ðə ˈwɛðə/
**Definition**: Feeling slightly ill or not as well as usual.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 身體唔太舒服；有啲唔精神。
**Example**: I'm feeling a bit under the weather today, so I might stay home.
**Cantonese Example**: 我今日覺得有啲唔舒服，所以可能留喺屋企。
