# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-09

Hi there! Today we're learning some useful British English words and phrases.

## 1. **Cheeky** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtʃiːki/
**Definition:** Impudent or disrespectful, typically in a playful or humorous way.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 厚面皮, 曳

**Example 1:**
He made a cheeky remark about my new haircut.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢對我嘅新髮型講咗句厚面皮嘅嘢。

**Example 2:**
Don't be so cheeky to your mother!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
唔好對你阿媽咁曳！

## 2. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed or upset.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望, 好沮喪

**Example 1:**
I was absolutely gutted when I failed the exam.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我考試唔合格，真係好失望。

**Example 2:**
She was gutted to hear that her favourite restaurant was closing down.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢聽到佢最鍾意嘅餐廳要執笠，覺得好沮喪。

## 3. **Knackered** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnækəd/
**Definition:** Very tired; exhausted.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好攰, 攰到散

**Example 1:**
I'm absolutely knackered after that long walk.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
行咗咁耐，我真係攰到散。

**Example 2:**
She looked completely knackered after working a double shift.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢返咗雙班，睇落去攰到暈。

## 4. **Sorted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsɔːtɪd/
**Definition:** Organized; arranged successfully. Also, dealt with successfully.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 搞掂, 安排好

**Example 1:**
Everything is sorted for the trip; we're ready to go.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
所有嘢都安排好晒，我哋準備好出發喇。

**Example 2:**
I've sorted out the problem with the computer.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我已經搞掂部電腦嘅問題喇。

## 5. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒi/
**Definition:** Suspicious, unreliable, or potentially dangerous.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑嘅, 危險嘅

**Example 1:**
That food looks a bit dodgy; I wouldn't eat it.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
啲嘢食睇落有啲可疑，我唔會食。

**Example 2:**
He's involved in some dodgy dealings.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢牽涉一啲危險嘅交易。

## 6. **Taking the mickey** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkɪŋ ðə ˈmɪki/
**Definition:** To tease or make fun of someone.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 玩嘢, 攞人嚟搞

**Example 1:**
Are you taking the mickey out of me?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你係咪玩嘢呀？

**Example 2:**
They were taking the mickey out of his accent.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢哋攞佢嘅口音嚟搞。

## 7. **Bits and bobs** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /bɪts ænd bɒbz/
**Definition:** Small miscellaneous items; odds and ends.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 雜物, 瑣碎嘢

**Example 1:**
I need to buy some bits and bobs for the house.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我需要買一啲雜物返屋企。

**Example 2:**
She keeps all her sewing bits and bobs in that box.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢將佢所有嘅縫紉瑣碎嘢擺喺嗰個盒入面。
