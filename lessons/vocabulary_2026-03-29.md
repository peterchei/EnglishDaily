# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-29

Hi there,

## 1. **Cheeky** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtʃiːki/
**Definition:** Impudent or presumptuous; showing a lack of respect in a way that is amusing rather than offensive.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 厚面皮 (hau5 min6 pei4), 串 (chuen3)

**Example 1:**
He made a cheeky remark about her new haircut.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢對佢嘅新髮型講咗句厚面皮嘅說話。

**Example 2:**
Don't be so cheeky with your grandmother!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
唔好對你嫲嫲咁串！

## 2. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望 (hou2 si1 mong6), 灰 (fui1)

**Example 1:**
I was absolutely gutted when I failed the exam.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我考試唔合格，真係好失望。

**Example 2:**
She was gutted to hear that her flight was cancelled.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢聽到佢嘅航班取消咗，覺得好灰。

## 3. **Queue** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /kjuː/
**Definition:** A line or sequence of people or vehicles awaiting their turn to be dealt with or to proceed.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 隊 (deui2), 排隊 (paai4 deui2)

**Example 1:**
There was a long queue at the bus stop this morning.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今朝喺巴士站有條好長嘅隊。

**Example 2:**
We had to queue for an hour to get into the museum.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我哋要排隊一個鐘頭先可以入博物館。

## 4. **To Fancy** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfænsi/
**Definition:** To have a feeling of attraction towards someone or something; to want something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 鍾意 (zung1 yi3), 想 (seung2)

**Example 1:**
Do you fancy going to the cinema tonight?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你今晚想唔想去睇戲啊？

**Example 2:**
I fancy a cup of tea.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我想飲杯茶。

## 5. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒi/
**Definition:** Suspicious; dishonest; potentially dangerous.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑 (ho2 yi4), 唔老實 (m4 lou5 sat6), 危險 (ngai4 him2)

**Example 1:**
That second-hand car looks a bit dodgy to me.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰部二手車睇落有啲可疑。

**Example 2:**
I wouldn't go down that alleyway, it looks a bit dodgy.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我唔會行落嗰條巷仔，睇落有啲危險。

## 6. **Cheers** (Exclamation/Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** (Exclamation) Used as a toast; (Noun) Thank you.
**Cantonese Meaning:** (敬酒) 飲杯 (yam2 bui1), (多謝) 多謝 (do1 ze6)

**Example 1:**
Cheers! To your health!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
飲杯！ 祝你身體健康！

**Example 2:**
"I'll get you a coffee." "Cheers!"
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"我幫你買杯咖啡。" "多謝！"

## 7. **Brilliant** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbrɪliənt/
**Definition:** Exceptionally clever or talented; Very good.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 叻 (lek1), 好好 (hou2 hou2)

**Example 1:**
She's a brilliant student and always gets top marks.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢係一個好叻嘅學生，成日攞最高分。

**Example 2:**
That's a brilliant idea!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個係個好好嘅主意！
