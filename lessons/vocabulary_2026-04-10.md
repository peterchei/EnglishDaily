# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-10

## N. **Reshuffling** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌriːˈʃʌflɪŋ/
**Definition:** The act of reorganizing or rearranging something, often involving changes in personnel or structure.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 重組，改組

**Example 1:**
The company is undergoing a major reshuffling of its management team.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
公司正進行管理團隊嘅重大重組。

**Example 2:**
The political landscape is experiencing a reshuffling after the recent election.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
最近嘅選舉之後，政治版圖正經歷改組。

## N. **Defame** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /dɪˈfeɪm/
**Definition:** To damage the good reputation of someone; to slander or libel.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 誹謗

**Example 1:**
She claimed the article was intended to defame her character.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢聲稱篇文章嘅目的係要誹謗佢嘅人格。

**Example 2:**
He threatened to sue the newspaper for attempting to defame him.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢威脅要控告報紙企圖誹謗佢。

## N. **Backlog** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbækˌlɒɡ/
**Definition:** An accumulation of uncompleted work or matters needing to be dealt with.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 積壓嘅工作

**Example 1:**
The hospital is struggling to clear the backlog of appointments caused by the pandemic.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
醫院正努力清理疫情導致嘅積壓預約。

**Example 2:**
The courts have a significant backlog of cases waiting to be heard.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
法院有大量積壓嘅案件等待審理。

## N. **Wagers** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈweɪdʒərz/
**Definition:** Something risked or staked on an uncertain event; bets.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 賭注

**Example 1:**
They placed wagers on the outcome of the football match.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢哋對足球比賽嘅結果落咗賭注。

**Example 2:**
He lost all his savings on a series of risky wagers.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢喺一連串高風險嘅賭注中輸晒所有嘅儲蓄。

## N. **Outcry** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈaʊtkraɪ/
**Definition:** A strong expression of public disapproval or anger.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 強烈抗議

**Example 1:**
The proposed changes to the law provoked a public outcry.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
擬議嘅法律修改引起咗公眾嘅強烈抗議。

**Example 2:**
There was an outcry after the company announced it would be closing the factory.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
公司宣布關閉工廠後，引起一片嘩然。

## N. **Line in the sand** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌlaɪn ɪn ðə ˈsænd/
**Definition:** A limit or boundary that, if crossed, will provoke a reaction or have consequences.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 底線

**Example 1:**
The government has drawn a line in the sand regarding further budget cuts.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府已經劃定咗關於進一步削減預算嘅底線。

**Example 2:**
He said that any personal insults would be a line in the sand.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢話任何個人侮辱都會係一條底線。
