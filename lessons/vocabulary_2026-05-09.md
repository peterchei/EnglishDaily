# Daily English Lesson - 2026-05-09

## 1. **Body Blow** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbɒdi bləʊ/
**Definition:** A serious setback or damaging event.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 沉重打擊

**Example 1:**
The election results were a body blow to the Labour party.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
選舉結果對工黨嚟講係一個沉重嘅打擊。

**Example 2:**
Losing the contract was a body blow to the company's finances.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
失去合約對公司嘅財政係一個沉重嘅打擊。

## 2. **Grim** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ɡrɪm/
**Definition:** Depressing or worrying to consider.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 令人沮喪嘅; 嚴峻嘅

**Example 1:**
The economic forecast for the next year looks grim.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
下一年嘅經濟預測睇落令人沮喪。

**Example 2:**
The hospital staff faced a grim situation with the increasing number of patients.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
醫院員工面對住病人數量不斷增加嘅嚴峻情況。

## 3. **Observe** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /əbˈzɜːv/
**Definition:** To watch carefully; to comply with.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 觀察; 遵守

**Example 1:**
Please observe the rules of the library.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
請遵守圖書館嘅規則。

**Example 2:**
Scientists are observing the effects of climate change on the polar ice caps.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
科學家們正在觀察氣候變化對極地冰蓋嘅影響。

## 4. **Reckless** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈrɛkləs/
**Definition:** Without thinking or caring about the consequences of an action.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 魯莽嘅; 不顧後果嘅

**Example 1:**
His reckless driving caused a serious accident.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢魯莽嘅駕駛導致咗一場嚴重嘅意外。

**Example 2:**
The company's reckless investments led to financial ruin.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
公司不顧後果嘅投資導致咗財政崩潰。

## 5. **Hovering** (Verb, as adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈhɒvərɪŋ/
**Definition:** Remaining in one place in the air; lingering near.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 盤旋; 徘徊

**Example 1:**
The helicopter was hovering over the building.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
直升機喺建築物上空盤旋。

**Example 2:**
She was hovering near the door, waiting for her friend.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢喺門口附近徘徊，等佢嘅朋友。

## 6. **Absurd** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /əbˈzɜːd/
**Definition:** Wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 荒謬嘅; 離譜嘅

**Example 1:**
It's absurd to think that he would agree to such a proposal.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
認為佢會同意咁樣嘅提議係荒謬嘅。

**Example 2:**
The play was so absurd that the audience couldn't stop laughing.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢齣劇太荒謬，觀眾忍唔住一直笑。

## 7. **Encrypted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪnˈkrɪptɪd/
**Definition:** Converted into a code to prevent unauthorized access.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 加密嘅

**Example 1:**
All sensitive data should be encrypted to protect it from hackers.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
所有敏感數據都應該加密，以保護佢哋免受黑客攻擊。

**Example 2:**
The message was encrypted, so I couldn't understand it.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
信息被加密咗，所以我睇唔明。
