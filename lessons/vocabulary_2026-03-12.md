# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-12

Hi there,

## 1. **Alright** (Adverb/Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ɔːlˈraɪt/
**Definition:** Satisfactory; acceptable; okay. Also used as a greeting similar to "Hello."
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好啦; 無問題; 你好

**Example 1:**
Are you alright after that fall?
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你跌倒後有無事呀？(nei5 dit3 dou2 hau6 jau5 mou5 si6 aa3?)

**Example 2:**
Alright, let's get started with the meeting.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
好啦，我哋開始開會啦。(hou2 laa1, ngo5 dei6 hoi1 chi2 hoi1 wui2 laa1.)

## 2. **Cheers** (Exclamation/Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** Used as a toast before drinking; also, informally, as a way of saying thank you or goodbye.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 乾杯; 多謝; 再見

**Example 1:**
Cheers to a successful project!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
祝我哋嘅項目成功！(zuk1 ngo5 dei6 ge3 hong6 muk6 sing4 gung1!)

**Example 2:**
"I'll hold the door for you." "Cheers!"
**Cantonese Example 2:**
“我幫你扶住度門。” “唔該晒！” (“ngo5 bong1 nei5 fu4 zyu6 dou6 mun4.” “m4 goi1 saai3!”)

## 3. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed or upset.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望; 好沮喪

**Example 1:**
I was gutted when I didn't get the job.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我冇得到份工嘅時候，我好失望。(ngo5 mou5 dak1 dou3 fan6 gung1 ge3 si4 hau6, ngo5 hou2 si1 mong6.)

**Example 2:**
She was absolutely gutted to hear the news.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢聽到個消息嘅時候，絕對好沮喪。(keoi5 ting1 dou2 go3 siu1 sik1 ge3 si4 hau6, zyut6 deoi3 hou2 jui2 song3.)

## 4. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒi/
**Definition:** Suspicious, unreliable, or potentially dangerous.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑嘅; 唔可靠嘅; 危險嘅

**Example 1:**
That restaurant looks a bit dodgy.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰間餐廳睇落有啲可疑。(go2 gaan1 caan1 teng1 tai2 lok6 jau5 di1 ho2 ji4.)

**Example 2:**
I wouldn't trust him; he's a bit dodgy.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我唔會相信佢，佢有啲唔可靠。(ngo5 m4 wui5 soeng1 seon3 keoi5, keoi5 jau5 di1 m4 ho2 kao3.)

## 5. **Taking the Piss** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkɪŋ ðə pɪs/
**Definition:** To tease or make fun of someone; to not be serious or respectful.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 玩嘢; 攞嚟搞; 唔認真

**Example 1:**
Are you taking the piss? I can't believe you actually did that!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你係咪玩嘢呀？ 我唔相信你真係咁做咗！(nei5 hai6 mai6 waan2 je5 aa3? ngo5 m4 soeng1 seon3 nei5 zan1 hai6 gam2 zou6 zo2!)

**Example 2:**
He's just taking the piss out of you, don't take him seriously.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢淨係玩你啫，唔使認真嘅。(keoi5 zing6 hai6 waan2 nei5 ze1, m4 sai2 jing6 zan1 ge3.)

## 6. **Sorted** (Adjective/Verb - past participle)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsɔːtɪd/
**Definition:** Organized; dealt with successfully; resolved.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 搞掂; 整好; 安排好

**Example 1:**
The problem is sorted now, so we can move on.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個問題搞掂咗喇，所以我哋可以繼續。(go3 man6 tai4 gaau2 dim6 zo2 laa3, so2 ji5 ngo5 dei6 ho2 ji5 gai3 zuk6.)

**Example 2:**
I've got my travel plans sorted.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我已經安排好晒我嘅旅行計劃。(ngo5 ji5 ging1 on1 paai4 hou2 saai3 ngo5 ge3 leoi5 haang4 gai3 waak6.)
