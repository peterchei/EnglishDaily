# Daily English Lesson - 2026-05-20

## 1. **Waiver** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈweɪvər/
**Definition:** An act or instance of waiving a right or claim.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 放棄權利聲明; 豁免

**Example 1:**
The UK issued a waiver on sanctions for certain Russian fuels.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
英國發出咗對某啲俄羅斯燃料嘅制裁豁免。

**Example 2:**
You will need to sign a waiver before participating in the activity.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
你參加活動之前需要簽署一份放棄權利聲明。

## 2. **Savour** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈseɪvər/
**Definition:** To enjoy or appreciate (something pleasant) completely, especially by dwelling on it.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 享受; 品味

**Example 1:**
Arsenal fans savoured every moment of their team's title win.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
阿仙奴球迷享受球隊贏得冠軍嘅每一刻。

**Example 2:**
Take your time to savour the delicious flavours of this dish.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
慢慢品味呢道菜嘅美味。

## 3. **Allegations** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌæləˈɡeɪʃənz/
**Definition:** A claim or assertion that someone has done something illegal or wrong, typically one made without proof.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 指控

**Example 1:**
The company is facing serious allegations of fraud.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司正面臨嚴重嘅詐騙指控。

**Example 2:**
She denied all the allegations made against her.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢否認所有對佢嘅指控。

## 4. **Bogus** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈboʊɡəs/
**Definition:** Not genuine or true; fake.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 虛假嘅; 冒牌嘅

**Example 1:**
The police warned people about bogus emails requesting personal information.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
警方警告市民提防要求提供個人資料嘅虛假電郵。

**Example 2:**
He was arrested for selling bogus tickets to the concert.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢因為賣演唱會嘅冒牌飛而被捕。

## 5. **Grip** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ɡrɪp/
**Definition:** Control or power over someone or something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 控制; 影響力

**Example 1:**
The president is trying to maintain his grip on the party.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
總統正試圖維持佢對黨嘅控制。

**Example 2:**
The cold weather has a firm grip on the country.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
寒冷嘅天氣對國家有強大嘅影響。

## 6. **Acknowledge** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /əkˈnɒlɪdʒ/
**Definition:** To accept or admit the truth or existence of (something).
**Cantonese Meaning:** 承認

**Example 1:**
The government acknowledged that the economy was slowing down.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府承認經濟正在放緩。

**Example 2:**
Please acknowledge receipt of this email.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
請確認收到呢封電郵。

## 7. **Projectile** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /prəˈdʒɛktaɪl/
**Definition:** An object fired from a gun with explosive propellant, or an object propelled through the air by any means.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 拋射物; 射彈

**Example 1:**
The soldier threw the projectile towards the enemy.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
士兵將拋射物扔向敵人。

**Example 2:**
Experts examined the projectile to determine its origin.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
專家檢查咗呢個射彈，以確定佢嘅來源。
