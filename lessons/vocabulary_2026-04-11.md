# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-11

## N. **Shelve** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ʃɛlv/
**Definition:** To decide not to proceed with something, either temporarily or permanently.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 擱置，暫緩，取消

**Example 1:**
The government decided to shelve the controversial plan due to public opposition.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府因為公眾反對而決定擱置有爭議嘅計劃。

**Example 2:**
We've had to shelve the project until we can secure more funding.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我哋要暫緩個項目，直到我哋可以獲得更多資金。

## N. **Bridge** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /brɪdʒ/
**Definition:** To connect or reduce the gap between two things, ideas, or groups of people.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 彌合，跨越，連接

**Example 1:**
Talks are aimed at bridging the gap between the two countries' positions.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
談判旨在彌合兩國立場之間嘅差距。

**Example 2:**
Education can help bridge the divide between rich and poor.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
教育可以幫助縮小貧富之間嘅差距。

## N. **Triumph** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtraɪʌmf/
**Definition:** A great victory or achievement.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 勝利，成就

**Example 1:**
The team celebrated their triumph in the championship.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
球隊慶祝佢哋喺錦標賽中嘅勝利。

**Example 2:**
Her election was a personal triumph after years of hard work.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
經過多年嘅努力，佢嘅當選係一個個人成就。

## N. **Obstacle** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɒbstəkəl/
**Definition:** Something that blocks one's way or prevents progress.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 障礙，阻礙

**Example 1:**
Lack of experience can be a significant obstacle to finding a job.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
缺乏經驗可能係搵工嘅一個重大障礙。

**Example 2:**
They overcame many obstacles to achieve their goal.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢哋克服咗好多障礙先達到佢哋嘅目標。

## N. **Ceasefire** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsiːsfaɪər/
**Definition:** An agreement to stop fighting, usually temporarily.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 停火

**Example 1:**
Negotiations are underway to establish a ceasefire.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
談判緊進行中，以達成停火協議。

**Example 2:**
The ceasefire has been broken several times.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
停火協議已經被打破咗好多次。

## N. **Coerce** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /kəʊˈɜːs/
**Definition:** To persuade someone to do something by using force or threats.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 強迫，脅迫

**Example 1:**
He was coerced into signing the contract.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢被強迫簽署咗合約。

**Example 2:**
You cannot coerce someone into making a confession.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
你唔可以強迫人認罪。

## N. **Pressure** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈprɛʃər/
**Definition:** The use of persuasion or force to make someone do something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 壓力，施壓

**Example 1:**
The company is under pressure to reduce costs.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
公司面臨降低成本嘅壓力。

**Example 2:**
They put pressure on him to resign.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢哋施壓要佢辭職。
