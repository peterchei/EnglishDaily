# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-06

Hi there!

## 1. **Queue** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /kjuː/
**Definition:** A line of people or vehicles waiting for something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 隊伍

**Example 1:**
There's a long queue outside the bakery this morning.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今朝麵包舖門口排咗條好長嘅隊。

**Example 2:**
We had to stand in the queue for over an hour to get tickets.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我哋要排隊超過一個鐘先買到飛。

## 2. **Cheers** (Exclamation)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** A toast of goodwill; informal way to say thank you or goodbye.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 多謝; 再見; 飲杯

**Example 1:**
Cheers! To a happy future!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
飲杯！祝大家有個快樂嘅將來！

**Example 2:**
"Thanks for your help." "Cheers!"
**Cantonese Example 2:**
「唔該你幫忙。」「唔駛客氣！」

## 3. **Rubbish** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈrʌbɪʃ/
**Definition:** Waste material; nonsense.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 垃圾; 胡說八道

**Example 1:**
Please put the rubbish in the bin.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
請將垃圾放入垃圾桶。

**Example 2:**
That's a load of rubbish! He's making it up.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
簡直係胡說八道！佢喺度作故仔。

## 4. **Brilliant** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbrɪlɪənt/
**Definition:** Exceptionally clever or talented; very bright or radiant.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 非常聰明; 燦爛

**Example 1:**
She's a brilliant student and always gets top marks.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢係個非常聰明嘅學生，成日攞最高分。

**Example 2:**
The sun was brilliant today.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
今日陽光好燦爛。

## 5. **Knackered** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnækəd/
**Definition:** Very tired; exhausted. (Informal)
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好攰

**Example 1:**
I'm absolutely knackered after that long walk.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
行咗咁耐，我真係好攰。

**Example 2:**
He looked completely knackered after working all night.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
通宵工作後，佢睇落好攰。

## 6. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed. (Informal)
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望

**Example 1:**
I was gutted when I didn't get the job.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我冇得到嗰份工，真係好失望。

**Example 2:**
She's gutted that she can't come to the party.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢唔可以嚟派對，佢覺得好失望。
