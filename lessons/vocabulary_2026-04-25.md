# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-25

Hello everyone, let's learn some new words today!

## 1. **Fraught** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /frɔːt/
**Definition:** Filled with or causing anxiety or concern; characterized by emotional distress or tension.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 充滿咗焦慮或者憂慮嘅；令人感到精神緊張嘅

**Example 1:**
The negotiations were fraught with difficulties and disagreements.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰啲談判充滿咗困難同分歧。

**Example 2:**
Her voice was fraught with emotion as she described the accident.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢形容嗰場意外嗰陣，把聲充滿咗情感。

## 2. **Sovereignty** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsɒvrənti/
**Definition:** Supreme power or authority; the authority of a state to govern itself or another state.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 最高權力或者權威；一個國家管治自己或者另一個國家嘅權力

**Example 1:**
The country fought for its sovereignty against foreign interference.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個國家為咗佢嘅主權對抗外國干預。

**Example 2:**
They debated the limits of national sovereignty in an increasingly globalized world.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢哋喺一個日益全球化嘅世界入面辯論國家主權嘅界限。

## 3. **Inmate** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɪnmeɪt/
**Definition:** A person confined to an institution such as a prison or hospital.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 被囚禁喺監獄或者醫院等機構嘅人

**Example 1:**
The prison houses over 500 inmates.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰間監獄關咗超過五百個囚犯。

**Example 2:**
He befriended several inmates during his time in prison.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢喺監獄嗰段時間同幾個囚犯成為咗朋友。

## 4. **Back Down** (Phrasal Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /bæk daʊn/
**Definition:** To withdraw or retract a claim or assertion, especially in the face of opposition.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 退一步；放棄主張或者聲明，尤其係面對反對嘅時候

**Example 1:**
The company was forced to back down after facing public pressure.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司喺面對公眾壓力之後被迫退一步。

**Example 2:**
Neither side was willing to back down from their demands.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
邊一方都唔願意放棄佢哋嘅要求。

## 5. **Perceived** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /pəˈsiːvd/
**Definition:** Understood or interpreted in a particular way; regarded as being.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 以某種方式理解或者詮釋；被認為係

**Example 1:**
The perceived threat to national security led to increased surveillance.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
對國家安全嘅潛在威脅導致咗加強監控。

**Example 2:**
Her perceived arrogance made her unpopular with her colleagues.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢被認為嘅傲慢令到佢唔受同事歡迎。

## 6. **Retaliation** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /rɪˌtæliˈeɪʃən/
**Definition:** Action taken in return for an injury or wrong; revenge.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 報復行為；為咗傷害或者錯誤而採取嘅行動；報仇

**Example 1:**
The company feared retaliation from its competitors.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司驚競爭對手報復。

**Example 2:**
He refused to testify, fearing retaliation against his family.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢拒絕作證，驚佢嘅家人受到報復。
