# Daily English Lesson - 2026-03-22

Hi there, today we'll learn some useful words and phrases.

## 1. **Cheers** (Exclamation)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** An informal expression used to mean "thank you," "goodbye," or "good luck."
**Cantonese Meaning:** 多謝, 再見, 祝你好運

**Example 1:**
"Cheers for helping me with the groceries!"
**Cantonese Example 1:**
"唔該幫我拎啲雜貨啊！"

**Example 2:**
"I'm off now, cheers!"
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"我走先啦，拜拜！"

## 2. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed or upset.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望, 好傷心

**Example 1:**
"I was absolutely gutted when I failed the exam."
**Cantonese Example 1:**
"我考試唔合格，真係好失望。"

**Example 2:**
"She was gutted to hear that her flight was cancelled."
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"佢聽到佢班機取消咗，好傷心。"

## 3. **Mug** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /mʌɡ/
**Definition:** To attack and rob (someone) in a public place.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 行劫, 打劫

**Example 1:**
"He was mugged on his way home from work."
**Cantonese Example 1:**
"佢放工返屋企嘅時候俾人打劫咗。"

**Example 2:**
"The police warned tourists to be careful of being mugged in that area."
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"警察警告遊客喺嗰個區域要小心俾人打劫。"

## 4. **Queue** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /kjuː/
**Definition:** A line of people or vehicles waiting their turn for something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 隊伍, 排隊

**Example 1:**
"There was a long queue outside the shop this morning."
**Cantonese Example 1:**
"今日朝早間舖頭出面排咗條長龍。"

**Example 2:**
"Please join the queue and wait for your turn."
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"請排隊等候。"

## 5. **Dodgy** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdɒdʒi/
**Definition:** Suspicious, unreliable, or potentially dangerous.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 可疑嘅, 唔可靠嘅, 危險嘅

**Example 1:**
"That area of town is a bit dodgy at night."
**Cantonese Example 1:**
"嗰個地區夜晚有啲危險。"

**Example 2:**
"I bought this watch from a dodgy-looking market stall."
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"我喺一個睇落唔太可靠嘅市場攤位買咗呢隻錶。"

## 6. **Taking the Piss** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkɪŋ ðə pɪs/
**Definition:** To tease or make fun of someone; to not take something seriously. (Informal)
**Cantonese Meaning:** 玩嘢, 攞嚟講笑, 唔認真

**Example 1:**
"Are you taking the piss? I can't believe you actually did that!"
**Cantonese Example 1:**
"你係咪玩嘢啊？ 我唔相信你真係咁做！"

**Example 2:**
"He's always taking the piss out of my accent."
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"佢成日攞我嘅口音嚟講笑。"

## 7. **Sorted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈsɔːtɪd/
**Definition:** Organized or dealt with successfully; resolved.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 搞掂, 整理好, 解決咗

**Example 1:**
"I've sorted out the problem with the computer."
**Cantonese Example 1:**
"我已經搞掂咗部電腦嘅問題。"

**Example 2:**
"Now that the accommodation is sorted, we can focus on the travel arrangements."
**Cantonese Example 2:**
"而家住宿搞掂咗，我哋可以專注喺行程安排上面。"
