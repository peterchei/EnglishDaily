## N. **Truce** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /truːs/
**Definition:** An agreement between enemies to stop fighting for a certain time.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 停戰協議 (ting4 zin3 seoi2 ji5)

**Example 1:**
The Easter truce in Ukraine has failed to bring lasting peace.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
烏克蘭嘅復活節停戰協議未能帶嚟持久嘅和平。
(wu1 hak1 laan4 ge3 fuk1 fat1 zit3 ting4 zin3 seoi2 ji5 mei6 nang4 daai3 lai4 ci4 gau2 ge3 wo4 ping4.)

**Example 2:**
Both sides agreed to a temporary truce to allow civilians to evacuate.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
雙方同意咗一個臨時停戰協議，俾平民可以撤離。
(seong1 fong1 tung4 ji3 zo2 jat1 go3 lam4 si4 ting4 zin3 seoi2 ji5, bei2 ping4 man4 ho2 ji5 che3 lei4.)

## N. **Disillusioned** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒənd/
**Definition:** Disappointed in someone or something that one discovers to be less good than one had believed.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 感到幻滅 (gam2 dou3 waan6 mit6)

**Example 1:**
Many disillusioned voters are looking for alternative political options.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
好多感到幻滅嘅選民正在尋找替代嘅政治選擇。
(ho2 do1 gam2 dou3 waan6 mit6 ge3 syun2 man4 jing6 zoi6 cam4 zaau2 tai3 doi6 ge3 zing3 zi6 syun2 zaak6.)

**Example 2:**
He became disillusioned with the company after years of broken promises.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺經歷咗多年嘅失信承諾之後，佢對間公司感到幻滅。
(hai2 ging1 lik6 zo2 do1 nin4 ge3 sat1 seon3 sing4 nok6 zi1 hau6, keoi5 deoi3 gaan1 gung1 si1 gam2 dou3 waan6 mit6.)

## N. **Propaganda** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌprɒpəˈɡændə/
**Definition:** Information, especially of a biased or misleading nature, used to promote a political cause or point of view.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 宣傳 (syun1 cyun4)

**Example 1:**
Experts warn that the viral videos are powerful propaganda.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
專家警告話，啲病毒式影片係強有力嘅宣傳。
(zyun1 gaa1 ging2 gou3 waa6, di1 beng6 duk6 sik1 ying2 pin2 hai6 koeng4 jau5 lik6 ge3 syun1 cyun4.)

**Example 2:**
The government was accused of using propaganda to sway public opinion.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
政府被指控利用宣傳嚟影響公眾輿論。
(zing3 fu2 bei6 zi2 hung3 lei6 jung6 syun1 cyun4 lai4 jing2 hoeng2 gung1 zung3 jyu4 leon6.)

## N. **Defiant** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /dɪˈfaɪənt/
**Definition:** Showing resistance or disobedience.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 挑衅嘅 (tiu1 san3 ge3) , 反抗嘅 (faan2 kong3 ge3)

**Example 1:**
The prime minister remained defiant despite the growing protests.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
儘管抗議活動日益增加，首相仍然保持挑衅嘅態度。
(zeon2 gun2 kong3 ji5 wut6 dung6 jat6 ji6 zeng1 gaa1, sau2 seong3 jing4 jin4 bo2 ci4 tiu1 san3 ge3 taai3 dou6.)

**Example 2:**
She gave a defiant look to her boss when he criticised her work.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
當佢老闆批評佢嘅工作時，佢俾咗個挑衅嘅眼神佢。
(dong1 keoi5 lou5 baan2 pai1 ping4 keoi5 ge3 gung1 zok3 si4, keoi5 bei2 zo2 go3 tiu1 san3 ge3 ngaan5 san4 keoi5.)

## N. **Voyage** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈvɔɪɪdʒ/
**Definition:** A long journey involving travel by sea or in space.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 航行 (hong4 hang4)

**Example 1:**
The astronauts completed a nine-day voyage around the Moon.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
宇航員完成咗一個九日嘅環繞月球航行。
(jyu5 hong4 jyun4 waan4 sing4 zo2 jat1 go3 gau2 jat6 ge3 waan4 jiu5 jyut6 kau4 hong4 hang4.)

**Example 2:**
They are planning a long sea voyage to explore remote islands.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢哋計劃緊一次長途海上航行，去探索偏遠嘅島嶼。
(keoi5 dei6 gai3 waak6 gan2 jat1 ci3 coeng4 tou4 hoi2 soeng6 hong4 hang4, heoi3 taam3 sok3 pin1 jyun5 ge3 dou2 seoi5.)

## N. **Probe** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /proʊb/
**Definition:** To investigate or examine thoroughly.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 調查 (diu6 caa4)

**Example 1:**
The prosecutor will probe the abuse claims against the candidate.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
檢察官將會調查針對該候選人嘅虐待指控。
(gim2 caat3 gun1 zoeng1 wui5 diu6 caa4 zeon1 deoi3 goi1 hau6 syun2 jan4 ge3 joek3 doi6 zi2 gung3.)

**Example 2:**
The police are probing the cause of the fire.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
警察正在調查火災嘅原因。
(ging2 caat3 zing3 zoi6 diu6 caa4 fo2 zoi1 ge3 jyun4 yan1.)
