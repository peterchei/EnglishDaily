# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-27

## 1. **Revitalise** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /riːˈvaɪtəlaɪz/
**Definition:** To give new life, energy, or success to something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 使…恢復生機; 使…復興; 使…振興

**Example 1:**
The government hopes to revitalise the city centre by attracting new businesses.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府希望通過吸引新嘅生意嚟振興市中心。

**Example 2:**
A fresh coat of paint can revitalise an old room.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
油上一層新油漆可以令舊嘅房間恢復生機。

## 2. **Directive** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /dɪˈrektɪv/
**Definition:** An official instruction or order.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 指示; 命令

**Example 1:**
The CEO issued a directive to cut costs across all departments.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
行政總裁發出指示，要求所有部門削減成本。

**Example 2:**
The soldiers followed the general's directive without question.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
士兵毫無疑問地遵從咗將軍嘅命令。

## 3. **Accused** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /əˈkjuːzd/
**Definition:** To charge someone with an offense or crime.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 指控; 控告

**Example 1:**
The company is accused of polluting the river with chemical waste.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司被指控用化學廢料污染條河。

**Example 2:**
She was wrongly accused of stealing the money.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢被錯誤咁指控偷咗啲錢。

## 4. **Packed-out** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈpækt ˈaʊt/
**Definition:** Extremely crowded; filled to capacity.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 擠滿咗嘅; 爆滿嘅

**Example 1:**
The concert was packed-out, with fans eager to see their favourite band.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個演唱會爆滿，粉絲們都好想睇佢哋最鍾意嘅樂隊。

**Example 2:**
The pub was packed-out on Friday night, making it difficult to find a seat.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
星期五晚間酒吧擠滿咗人，好難搵到位坐。

## 5. **Delegate** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈdelɪɡət/
**Definition:** A person chosen or elected to represent a group.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 代表

**Example 1:**
Each country sent a delegate to the international conference.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
每個國家都派咗一個代表去國際會議。

**Example 2:**
The union delegate spoke on behalf of the workers.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
工會代表代表工人發言。

## 6. **Manifesto** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌmænɪˈfestəʊ/
**Definition:** A public declaration of policies or intentions.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 宣言; 聲明

**Example 1:**
The political party released their manifesto ahead of the election.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個政黨喺選舉前發表咗佢哋嘅宣言。

**Example 2:**
The artist's manifesto outlined their vision for a new artistic movement.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
藝術家嘅宣言概述咗佢哋對新藝術運動嘅願景。

## 7. **Thudding** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈθʌdɪŋ/
**Definition:** Making a dull, heavy sound.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 發出沉悶聲嘅; 咚咚聲嘅

**Example 1:**
We heard the thudding sound of the boxes being dropped downstairs.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我哋聽到啲箱喺樓下掉落嚟嘅咚咚聲。

**Example 2:**
The music had a heavy thudding beat that shook the floor.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
啲音樂有種沉重嘅咚咚聲節拍，震動咗地板。
