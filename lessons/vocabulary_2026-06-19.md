# Daily English Lesson - 2026-06-19

## 1. **Turning point** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtɜː.nɪŋ pɔɪnt/
**Definition:** A time at which a decisive change in a situation occurs, especially one with beneficial results.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 轉折點

**Example 1:**
The mayor believes his recent election victory will be a turning point for the local economy.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
市長相信他最近的選舉勝利將會是當地經濟的一個轉折點。

**Example 2:**
Winning the championship was a turning point in her sporting career.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
贏得錦標賽是她運動生涯的一個轉折點。

## 2. **Desperation** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌdes.pəˈreɪ.ʃən/
**Definition:** A state of despair, typically one which results in rash or extreme behavior.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 絕望；孤注一擲

**Example 1:**
The leader claimed the trade deal was signed out of pure desperation.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
那位領袖聲稱這項貿易協議是在極度絕望下簽署的。

**Example 2:**
In a moment of desperation, she sold her most prized possession to pay the bills.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
在絕望之際，她賣掉了最珍貴的財物來支付帳單。

## 3. **Inescapable** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌɪn.ɪˈskeɪ.pə.bəl/
**Definition:** Unable to be avoided or ignored.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 無法逃避的；不可避免的

**Example 1:**
The government faces the inescapable truth that public opinion has shifted against them.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府面臨著一個無法逃避的事實，即公眾輿論已經轉向反對他們。

**Example 2:**
The inescapable logic of his argument left the committee with no other choice.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
他論點中不可避免的邏輯讓委員會別無選擇。

## 4. **Empowered** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪmˈpaʊ.əd/
**Definition:** Having the knowledge, confidence, or legal authority to do something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 獲得授權的；有自主權的

**Example 1:**
Local communities feel more empowered when they are involved in the decision-making process.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
當社區參與決策過程時，他們會感到更有自主權。

**Example 2:**
The new policy has empowered employees to manage their own working hours.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
這項新政策賦予了員工管理自己工作時間的權力。

## 5. **Refinery** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /rɪˈfaɪ.nər.i/
**Definition:** A factory where substances in their natural state, such as oil or sugar, are made pure.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 煉油廠；精煉廠

**Example 1:**
Smoke was seen rising from the oil refinery after the drone strike.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
無人機襲擊後，人們看到煉油廠冒出濃煙。

**Example 2:**
The company is investing millions to upgrade its sugar refinery.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
該公司正投資數百萬美元升級其糖精煉廠。

## 6. **Insurgency** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪnˈsɜː.dʒən.si/
**Definition:** An active revolt or uprising against an authority, especially a government.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 叛亂；起義

**Example 1:**
The military has been struggling to contain the militant insurgency in the northern region.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
軍方一直努力遏制該北部地區的武裝叛亂。

**Example 2:**
After years of insurgency, the country is finally moving towards a fragile peace.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
經過多年的叛亂，這個國家終於邁向脆弱的和平。

## 7. **Anticipated** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ænˈtɪs.ɪ.peɪ.tɪd/
**Definition:** Happening, or expected to happen, in the near future.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 備受期待的；預期的

**Example 1:**
The release of the highly anticipated film was delayed until the summer.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
這部備受期待的電影上映日期被延遲到夏天。

**Example 2:**
The results were better than the financial analysts had anticipated.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
結果比金融分析師預期的要好。
