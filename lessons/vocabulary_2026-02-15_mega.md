1. **Oyster Card** (Noun)
**Pronunciation**: /ˈɔɪstər kɑːrd/
**Definition**: A smart card used for electronic payment on public transport in Greater London.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 蠔卡（倫敦交通儲值卡，類似香港嘅八達通）。
**Example 1**: Don't forget to top up your Oyster card before the morning rush.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 記得喺晨早繁忙時間之前幫你張蠔卡增值呀。
**Example 2**: You can use your Oyster card on buses, the Tube, and even some river boats.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 你可以用蠔卡搭巴士、地鐵，甚至一啲河上渡輪都得。

2. **The City** (Noun Phrase)
**Pronunciation**: /ðə ˈsɪti/
**Definition**: Specifically refers to the financial district of London (also known as the Square Mile).
**Cantonese Explanation**: 倫敦市中心金融區（指 "Square Mile"，唔係指成個倫敦）。
**Example 1**: He works for a large investment bank in the City.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 佢喺倫敦金融區一間大型投資銀行做嘢。
**Example 2**: The architecture in the City is a mix of historic buildings and modern skyscrapers.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 金融區嘅建築融合咗歷史古蹟同埋現代摩天大樓。

3. **Stark** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation**: /stɑːrk/
**Definition**: Severe or bare in appearance or outline; or used to describe a sharp contrast.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 鮮明嘅、嚴峻嘅、赤裸裸嘅。常用於形容對比非常大。
**Example 1**: There is a stark contrast between the rich and poor areas of the city.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 呢座城市嘅富裕地區同貧窮地區之間有鮮明嘅對比。
**Example 2**: The white walls provided a stark background for the colorful paintings.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 白色嘅牆壁為色彩繽紛嘅畫作提供咗一個鮮明嘅背景。

4. **Commute** (Noun/Verb)
**Pronunciation**: /kəˈmjuːt/
**Definition**: To travel some distance between one's home and place of work on a regular basis.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 通勤、往返屋企同公司。
**Example 1**: My daily commute takes about 45 minutes by train.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 我每日搭火車返工放工大約要 45 分鐘。
**Example 2**: Many people choose to commute from outside London to save on rent.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 好多人都選擇喺倫敦市外通勤，為咗慳返啲租金。

5. **In the red** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation**: /ɪn ðə red/
**Definition**: To owe money to the bank; to have a negative balance.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 赤字、負債、戶口見紅。
**Example 1**: After buying those expensive theatre tickets, my account is now in the red.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 買咗果啲咁貴嘅劇院飛之後，我個戶口而家見紅喇。
**Example 2**: The company has been in the red for three consecutive quarters.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 呢間公司已經連續三個季度出現赤字。

6. **Off the beaten track** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation**: /ɒf ðə ˈbiːtn træk/
**Definition**: In a place where few people go; far from the main tourist areas.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 偏僻、少人去嘅地方、非主流景點。
**Example 1**: We found a lovely little cafe that was slightly off the beaten track.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 我哋搵到一間好得意嘅細咖啡店，位置有啲偏僻。
**Example 2**: If you want a quiet holiday, try going somewhere off the beaten track.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 如果你想過個寧靜嘅假期，可以試下發掘一啲非主流嘅景點。

7. **A bit of a trek** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation**: /ə bɪt əv ə trek/
**Definition**: A journey that is surprisingly long or difficult.
**Cantonese Explanation**: 有排去、段路好遠、幾轉折。
**Example 1**: Getting to that park from here is a bit of a trek on the bus.
**Cantonese Example 1**: 由呢度搭巴士去果個公園都有排去呀。
**Example 2**: I'd love to visit, but your new house is a bit of a trek from the station.
**Cantonese Example 2**: 我好想去探你，不過你間新屋離車站真係幾遠下。
