# Daily English Lesson - 2026-05-21

## 1. **Intercept** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌɪntəˈsɛpt/
**Definition:** To stop or seize (someone or something) while on the way somewhere.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 攔截

**Example 1:**
The fighter jets were scrambled to intercept the enemy bomber.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
戰鬥機緊急起飛去攔截敵方轟炸機。

**Example 2:**
The police managed to intercept the package before it reached its destination.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
警察成功喺包裹到達目的地之前攔截咗佢。

## 2. **Scheme** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /skiːm/
**Definition:** A large-scale systematic plan or arrangement for attaining some particular object or putting a particular idea into effect.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 計劃, 方案

**Example 1:**
The government launched a new scheme to help young people find jobs.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府推出咗一項新嘅計劃去幫助年輕人揾工。

**Example 2:**
He devised a clever scheme to avoid paying taxes.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢諗出咗一個聰明嘅計劃去避免交稅。

## 3. **Taunting** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtɔːntɪŋ/
**Definition:** Provoking or challenging (someone) with insulting remarks.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 嘲諷, 挑釁

**Example 1:**
The bullies were taunting the smaller boy in the playground.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
啲惡霸喺遊樂場度嘲諷個細路仔。

**Example 2:**
He regretted taunting his opponent after winning the game.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢後悔喺贏咗比賽之後嘲諷佢嘅對手。

## 4. **Tycoon** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /taɪˈkuːn/
**Definition:** A wealthy, powerful person in business or industry.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 大亨, 巨頭

**Example 1:**
The property tycoon made his fortune in real estate.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
個地產大亨靠房地產發咗達。

**Example 2:**
She is a media tycoon with interests in newspapers and television.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢係一個媒體巨頭，喺報紙同電視方面都有利益。

## 5. **Fare-free** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈfeə friː/
**Definition:** Without a charge for travel; free of fares.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 免費嘅車費

**Example 1:**
The city is considering introducing fare-free public transport for residents.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
呢個城市考慮為居民引入免費公共交通。

**Example 2:**
A fare-free bus service operates in the town centre.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
市中心有一項免費巴士服務營運。

## 6. **Manoeuvre** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /məˈnuːvə/
**Definition:** A movement or series of moves requiring skill and care.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 操作, 手法, 策略

**Example 1:**
The pilot performed a difficult manoeuvre to land the plane safely.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
機師做咗一個困難嘅操作去安全降落架飛機。

**Example 2:**
The politician used a clever manoeuvre to win the debate.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
個政治家用咗一個聰明嘅策略去贏咗場辯論。

## 7. **Placements** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈpleɪsmənts/
**Definition:** The action of placing someone or something in a particular place or situation. In this context, referring to children being placed in care facilities.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 安置, 安排位置

**Example 1:**
The social worker is responsible for finding suitable placements for children in care.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
社工負責為受照顧兒童搵到合適嘅安置。

**Example 2:**
There is a shortage of foster care placements in the region.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺呢個地區寄養家庭嘅安置位置短缺。
