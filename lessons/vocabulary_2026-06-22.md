# Daily English Lesson - 2026-06-22

Hello everyone! Let's look at some useful vocabulary from today's headlines.

## 1. **Brink** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /brɪŋk/
**Definition:** A point at which something, typically something unwelcome or difficult, is about to happen.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 邊緣；緊要關頭

**Example 1:**
The country is on the brink of a major political change.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
呢個國家正處於重大政治變革嘅邊緣。

**Example 2:**
The company was on the brink of collapse before the new investment arrived.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
喺新資金注入之前，間公司差啲就面臨倒閉。

## 2. **Binding** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbaɪndɪŋ/
**Definition:** An agreement or promise that must be followed because it is legal or official.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 具約束力嘅；法律上必須執行嘅

**Example 1:**
The preliminary election results are not yet legally binding.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
初步選舉結果目前仲未具備法律約束力。

**Example 2:**
Please read the contract carefully before signing this binding agreement.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
簽署呢份具約束力嘅合約之前，請仔細閱讀。

## 3. **Stronghold** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈstrɒŋ.həʊld/
**Definition:** A place where a particular cause or belief is strongly defended or upheld, often used in military contexts.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 據點；堡壘

**Example 1:**
The city is considered a vital stronghold for the defending army.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
呢座城市被視為防守軍嘅重要據點。

**Example 2:**
The political party has a stronghold in the northern regions of the country.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個政黨喺該國北部地區擁有強大嘅據點。

## 4. **Outstrip** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌaʊtˈstrɪp/
**Definition:** To exceed or be greater than something, usually in terms of supply or demand.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 超過；勝過

**Example 1:**
Ticket demand for the concert continues to outstrip supply.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
演唱會門票嘅需求持續超過供應。

**Example 2:**
Economic growth in the region has begun to outstrip expectations.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
該地區嘅經濟增長已經開始超出預期。

## 5. **Consent** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /kənˈsent/
**Definition:** Permission for something to happen or agreement to do something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 同意；准許

**Example 1:**
The report argues that tech companies must prioritize user consent regarding personal images.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
該報告指出，科技公司必須優先考慮用戶關於個人影像嘅同意權。

**Example 2:**
He took the car without the owner's consent.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢未經車主同意就攞走咗架車。

## 6. **In a bid to** (Phrase)
**Pronunciation:** /ɪn ə bɪd tuː/
**Definition:** In an attempt to achieve or obtain something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 為了嘗試；企圖

**Example 1:**
The company is offering free services in a bid to attract new customers.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰間公司提供免費服務，係為了吸引新顧客。

**Example 2:**
The government changed the law in a bid to reduce extreme heat risks.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
政府修改咗法律，係為了嘗試減少極端高溫帶嚟嘅風險。
