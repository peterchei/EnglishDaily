# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-03

Hi there! Let's learn some English.

## 1. **Queue** (Noun/Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /kjuː/
**Definition:** A line of people waiting their turn for something. To form a line while waiting.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 隊 (deui2), 排隊 (paai4 deui2)

**Example 1:**
There was a long queue outside the bakery this morning.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今朝麵包鋪出面排咗條好長嘅隊。
(Gam1 ziu1 min6 baau1 pou3 ceot1 min6 paai4 zo2 tiu4 hou2 coeng4 ge3 deui2.)

**Example 2:**
Please queue up in an orderly fashion.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
請大家有秩序噉排隊。
(Cing2 daai6 ga1 jau5 zi6 zeoi6 gam2 paai4 deui2.)

## 2. **Cheers** (Exclamation)
**Pronunciation:** /tʃɪəz/
**Definition:** An informal expression used to mean "thank you", "goodbye", or a toast before drinking.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 多謝 (do1 ze6), 拜拜 (baai1 baai3), 乾杯 (gon1 bui1)

**Example 1:**
"I'll hold the door for you." "Cheers!"
**Cantonese Example 1:**
「我幫你揸住道門。」「多謝！」
( "Ngo5 bong1 nei5 zaa1 zyu6 dou6 mun4." "Do1 ze6!")

**Example 2:**
Cheers, see you next week!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
拜拜，下個禮拜見！
(Baai1 baai3, haa6 go3 lai5 baai3 gin3!)

## 3. **Lovely** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈlʌvli/
**Definition:** Beautiful; very pleasing.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好靚 (hou2 leng3), 好正 (hou2 zeng3), 好好 (hou2 hou2)

**Example 1:**
What a lovely day!
**Cantonese Example 1:**
今日天氣真係好好啊！
(Gam1 jat6 tin1 hei3 zan1 hai6 hou2 hou2 aa3!)

**Example 2:**
She has a lovely singing voice.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢把聲唱歌唱得好好聽。
(Keoi5 baa2 seng1 coeng3 go1 coeng3 dak1 hou2 hou2 teng1.)

## 4. **Brilliant** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbrɪliənt/
**Definition:** Exceptionally clever or talented; very bright or radiant.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 叻 (lek1), 好勁 (hou2 ging6), 好聰明 (hou2 cung1 ming4)

**Example 1:**
She's a brilliant scientist.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢係一個好叻嘅科學家。
(Keoi5 hai6 jat1 go3 hou2 lek1 ge3 fo1 hok6 gaa1.)

**Example 2:**
That's a brilliant idea!
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢個主意真係好正！
(Ni1 go3 zyu2 yi3 zan1 hai6 hou2 zeng3!)

## 5. **Gutted** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɡʌtɪd/
**Definition:** Extremely disappointed; devastated.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 好失望 (hou2 sat1 mong6), 好傷心 (hou2 seung1 sam1)

**Example 1:**
I was gutted when I didn't get the job.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我冇攞到份工，真係好失望。
(Ngo5 mou5 lo2 dou2 fan6 gung1, zan1 hai6 hou2 sat1 mong6.)

**Example 2:**
He was gutted to hear about his friend's accident.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢聽到佢朋友嘅意外，覺得好傷心。
(Keoi5 teng1 dou2 keoi5 pang4 jau5 ge3 ji3 ngoi6, gok3 dak1 hou2 seung1 sam1.)

## 6. **Takeaway** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈteɪkəweɪ/
**Definition:** A meal bought at a restaurant or shop and eaten elsewhere.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 外賣 (ngoi6 maai6)

**Example 1:**
Let's get a takeaway tonight.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我哋今晚叫外賣啦。
(Ngo5 dei6 gam1 maan1 giu3 ngoi6 maai6 laa1.)

**Example 2:**
I fancy a Chinese takeaway.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我想食中式外賣。
(Ngo5 soeng2 sik6 zung1 sik1 ngoi6 maai6.)
