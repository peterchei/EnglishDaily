# Daily English Lesson - 2026-04-13

## N. **Oust** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /aʊst/
**Definition:** To drive out or expel (someone) from a position or place.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 驅逐, 趕走

**Example 1:**
The citizens voted to oust the corrupt leader from power.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
市民投票將腐敗嘅領導人趕落台。

**Example 2:**
The company was forced to oust the CEO after the scandal.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
公司因為醜聞被迫將行政總裁革職。

## N. **Legislation** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌledʒɪˈsleɪʃn/
**Definition:** Laws or a set of laws suggested by a government and made official by a parliament.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 法例, 立法

**Example 1:**
The new legislation aims to reduce carbon emissions.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
新嘅法例旨在減少碳排放。

**Example 2:**
Parliament is debating the proposed legislation on immigration.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
議會正在辯論有關移民嘅擬議立法。

## N. **Referendum** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌrefəˈrendəm/
**Definition:** A general vote by the electorate on a single political question which has been referred to them for a direct decision.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 公民投票

**Example 1:**
The government held a referendum on whether to leave the European Union.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府就脫離歐盟舉行咗一次公民投票。

**Example 2:**
The proposal will be put to a referendum next year.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢個提案將會喺下年進行公民投票。

## N. **Blockade** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /blɒˈkeɪd/
**Definition:** An act or means of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 封鎖

**Example 1:**
The navy imposed a blockade on the enemy port.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
海軍對敵方港口實施封鎖。

**Example 2:**
The trade blockade has severely damaged the country's economy.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
貿易封鎖嚴重損害咗國家嘅經濟。

## N. **Negotiations** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /nɪˌɡəʊʃiˈeɪʃənz/
**Definition:** Discussions aimed at reaching an agreement.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 談判

**Example 1:**
The peace negotiations broke down after several days.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
和平談判喺幾日之後破裂。

**Example 2:**
The company is in negotiations with the union over pay and conditions.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
公司正同工會就工資同埋工作條件進行談判。

## N. **Gubernatorial** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˌɡuːbənəˈtɔːriəl/
**Definition:** Relating to a governor or governorship.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 州長嘅

**Example 1:**
The gubernatorial election is expected to be very close.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
預計州長選舉會非常接近。

**Example 2:**
He announced his candidacy for the gubernatorial race.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
佢宣布參選州長。

## N. **Avatar** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈævətɑːr/
**Definition:** An icon or figure representing a particular person in video games, Internet forums, etc.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 頭像, 分身

**Example 1:**
She created a digital avatar to represent herself in the virtual world.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
佢創造咗一個數碼頭像去代表佢自己喺虛擬世界入面。

**Example 2:**
The dancer used a digital avatar to perform after losing her physical abilities.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
呢位舞蹈員喺失去咗身體能力之後，用數碼分身嚟表演。
