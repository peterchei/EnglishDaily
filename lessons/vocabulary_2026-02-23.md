# Daily English Lesson - 2026-02-23

## 1. **Imminent** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈɪm.ɪ.nənt/
**Definition:** Coming or likely to happen very soon.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 即將來臨的 / 逼近的

**Example 1:**
The company announced that their merger with the competitor is imminent.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
公司宣佈與競爭對手的合併即將發生。

**Example 2:**
Dark clouds filled the sky, signaling an imminent storm.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
烏雲滿佈天空，預示著暴風雨即將來臨。

---

## 2. **Mitigate** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪt/
**Definition:** To make something less severe, harmful, or painful.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 減輕 / 緩和 / 緩解

**Example 1:**
Planting more trees can help mitigate the effects of climate change.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
種植更多樹木有助於減緩氣候變化的影響。

**Example 2:**
The government introduced new policies to mitigate the economic crisis.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
政府推出了新政策以緩解經濟危機。

---

## 3. **Ambivalent** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /æmˈbɪv.ə.lənt/
**Definition:** Having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something or someone.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 心情矛盾的 / 模稜兩可的

**Example 1:**
She remained ambivalent about accepting the job offer in another country.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
她對於是否接受那份國外的工作錄取仍然感到矛盾。

**Example 2:**
Voters are ambivalent towards the new tax reform proposal.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
選民對新的稅制改革提案持矛盾態度。

---

## 4. **Conducive** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /kənˈdjuː.sɪv/
**Definition:** Making a certain situation or outcome likely or possible.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 有利於...的 / 有助於...的

**Example 1:**
A quiet environment is conducive to studying effectively.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
安靜的環境有利於有效學習。

**Example 2:**
Soft lighting creates an atmosphere conducive to relaxation.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
柔和的燈光營造出有助於放鬆的氛圍。

---

## 5. **Hit the nail on the head** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation:** /hɪt ðə neɪl ɒn ðə hed/
**Definition:** To describe exactly what is causing a situation or problem; to be exactly right.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 一針見血 / 說得完全正確

**Example 1:**
You really hit the nail on the head with your analysis of the problem.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你對問題的分析真是一針見血。

**Example 2:**
When he said we need better communication, he hit the nail on the head.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
當他說我們需要更好的溝通時，他說得完全正確。

---

## 6. **Take with a grain of salt** (Idiom)
**Pronunciation:** /teɪk wɪð ə ɡreɪn ɒv sɒlt/
**Definition:** To view something with skepticism or not to interpret something literally.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 半信半疑 / 不完全相信 / 持保留態度

**Example 1:**
You should take everything you read on social media with a grain of salt.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
你應該對社交媒體上讀到的所有內容持保留態度。

**Example 2:**
His stories are entertaining, but I usually take them with a grain of salt.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
他的故事很有趣，但我通常都半信半疑。

---

## 7. **Nuance** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnjuː.ɑːns/
**Definition:** A subtle difference in or shade of meaning, expression, or sound.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 細微差別 / 微妙之處

**Example 1:**
Understanding the nuance of tone is important in effective communication.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
理解語氣的細微差別對於有效溝通很重要。

**Example 2:**
The translator worked hard to capture every nuance of the original poem.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
翻譯者努力捕捉原詩的每一個微妙之處。
