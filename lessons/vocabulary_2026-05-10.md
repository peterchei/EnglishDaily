# Daily English Lesson - 2026-05-10

## 1. **Trigger** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtrɪɡər/
**Definition:** To cause something to begin or happen.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 引發, 觸發

**Example 1:**
The announcement triggered a wave of protests across the country.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰個宣佈喺全國引發咗一陣示威浪潮。

**Example 2:**
Certain foods can trigger her allergies.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
某啲食物會觸發佢嘅敏感。

## 2. **Poised** (Adjective)
**Pronunciation:** /pɔɪzd/
**Definition:** Ready and prepared for something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 準備好嘅, 蓄勢待發嘅

**Example 1:**
The company is poised to launch its new product next week.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司準備好下個禮拜推出佢哋嘅新產品。

**Example 2:**
The athlete was poised at the starting line, ready to run.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰個運動員喺起跑線準備好，準備跑步。

## 3. **Showcase** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈʃoʊkeɪs/
**Definition:** To display or present something in an attractive or favourable way.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 展示, 展現

**Example 1:**
The exhibition will showcase the artist's latest works.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰個展覽將會展示嗰位藝術家嘅最新作品。

**Example 2:**
The company used the conference to showcase its new technology.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
間公司利用咗嗰個會議去展現佢哋嘅新科技。

## 4. **Condemn** (Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /kənˈdɛm/
**Definition:** To express strong disapproval of something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 譴責

**Example 1:**
The government condemned the attack as an act of terrorism.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
政府譴責嗰次襲擊係恐怖主義行為。

**Example 2:**
Human rights groups condemned the use of excessive force by the police.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
人權組織譴責警察使用過度武力。

## 5. **Strikes** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /straɪks/
**Definition:** Attacks, especially military attacks.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 襲擊, 攻擊

**Example 1:**
The air strikes targeted military installations.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰啲空襲嘅目標係軍事設施。

**Example 2:**
Recent strikes have caused significant damage to the infrastructure.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
最近嘅襲擊對基礎設施造成咗嚴重破壞。

## 6. **By-election** (Noun)
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbaɪ ɪˈlɛkʃən/
**Definition:** An election held to fill a political office that has become vacant between general elections.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 補選

**Example 1:**
The by-election will be held next month.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
嗰次補選將會喺下個月舉行。

**Example 2:**
The result of the by-election could indicate a shift in public opinion.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
嗰次補選嘅結果可能顯示公眾輿論嘅轉變。

## 7. **Scale back** (Phrasal Verb)
**Pronunciation:** /skeɪl bæk/
**Definition:** To reduce in extent or amount.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 縮減, 減少

**Example 1:**
The company decided to scale back its operations in Europe.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
間公司決定縮減佢喺歐洲嘅業務。

**Example 2:**
Due to budget cuts, we have to scale back the project.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
由於預算削減，我哋需要縮減嗰個項目。
