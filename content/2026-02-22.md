# Daily English Lesson - 2026-02-22

## 1. Catch up (Phrasal Verb)
**Meaning:** To talk to someone you have not seen for a while to find out what they have been doing.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 敘舊 / 了解近況

**Example 1:**
Let's meet for coffee and catch up.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我哋約出嚟飲杯咖啡敘舊啦。

**Example 2:**
I have a lot of work to catch up on.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我有好多工作要趕返進度。

## 2. Play it by ear (Idiom)
**Meaning:** To decide how to deal with a situation as it develops, rather than having a plan.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 見步行步 / 隨機應變

**Example 1:**
We don't have a schedule for the trip, we'll just play it by ear.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我哋個旅程冇行程表，到時見步行步啦。

**Example 2:**
I'm not sure how long the meeting will last, so let's play it by ear for dinner.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我唔確定個會要開幾耐，晚飯嗰陣再隨機應變啦。

## 3. Burn out (Phrasal Verb)
**Meaning:** To become extremely tired or ill by working too hard over a period of time.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 耗盡精力 / 做到殘

**Example 1:**
If you keep working these long hours, you're going to burn out.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
如果你繼續長時間工作，你會做到殘㗎。

**Example 2:**
She felt completely burned out after the big project.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
做完個大項目之後，佢覺得精力耗盡。

## 4. Get the hang of (Idiom)
**Meaning:** To learn how to do something or use something properly.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 上手 / 掌握竅門

**Example 1:**
It takes a while to get the hang of driving a manual car.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
學揸棍波車要一段時間先上手。

**Example 2:**
Don't worry, you'll get the hang of the new software soon.
**Cantonese Example 2:**
唔駛擔心，你會好快掌握到新軟件嘅用法。

## 5. In the loop (Idiom)
**Meaning:** To be part of a group of people who have information about something.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 知情 / 收風

**Example 1:**
Please keep me in the loop regarding the project status.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
請你要話俾我知個項目嘅最新情況。

**Example 2:**
I've been out of the loop for a while, what happened?
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我脫節咗一段時間，發生咩事？

## 6. Call it a day (Idiom)
**Meaning:** To decide to stop working, especially because you have done enough or you are tired.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 收工 / 到此為止

**Example 1:**
We've been working for 10 hours, let's call it a day.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我哋做咗十個鐘啦，收工啦。

**Example 2:**
I think we've done enough for now, shall we call it a day?
**Cantonese Example 2:**
我覺得我哋做得差唔多啦，不如今日到此為止？

## 7. Hit the sack (Idiom)
**Meaning:** To go to bed.
**Cantonese Meaning:** 瞓覺

**Example 1:**
I'm exhausted, I'm going to hit the sack.
**Cantonese Example 1:**
我好攰，我去瞓覺啦。

**Example 2:**
What time do you usually hit the sack on weekends?
**Cantonese Example 2:**
你週末通常幾點瞓覺？
