Imminent (Adjective) /ˈɪm.ɪ.nənt/

Coming or likely to happen very soon.

廣東話：即將來臨的 / 逼近的
The company announced that their merger with the competitor is imminent.
公司宣佈與競爭對手的合併即將發生。
Mitigate (Verb) /ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪt/

To make something less severe, harmful, or painful.

廣東話：減輕 / 緩和 / 緩解
Planting more trees can help mitigate the effects of climate change.
種植更多樹木有助於減緩氣候變化的影響。
Ambivalent (Adjective) /æmˈbɪv.ə.lənt/

Having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something or someone.

廣東話：心情矛盾的 / 模稜兩可的
She remained ambivalent about accepting the job offer in another country.
她對於是否接受那份國外的工作錄取仍然感到矛盾。
Conducive (Adjective) /kənˈdjuː.sɪv/

Making a certain situation or outcome likely or possible.

廣東話：有利於...的 / 有助於...的
A quiet environment is conducive to studying effectively.
安靜的環境有利於有效學習。
Hit the nail on the head (Idiom) /hɪt ðə neɪl ɒn ðə hed/

To describe exactly what is causing a situation or problem; to be exactly right.

廣東話：一針見血 / 說得完全正確
You really hit the nail on the head with your analysis of the problem.
你對問題的分析真是一針見血。
Take with a grain of salt (Idiom) /teɪk wɪð ə ɡreɪn ɒv sɒlt/

To view something with skepticism or not to interpret something literally.

廣東話：半信半疑 / 不完全相信 / 持保留態度
You should take everything you read on social media with a grain of salt.
你應該對社交媒體上讀到的所有內容持保留態度。
Nuance (Noun) /ˈnjuː.ɑːns/

A subtle difference in or shade of meaning, expression, or sound.

廣東話：細微差別 / 微妙之處
Understanding the nuance of tone is important in effective communication.
理解語氣的細微差別對於有效溝通很重要。
